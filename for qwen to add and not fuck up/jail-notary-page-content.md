## Oklahoma DOC Requirements for Visiting Notaries

The Oklahoma Department of Corrections maintains strict requirements for all visitors to state correctional facilities, including notaries. Understanding these requirements is essential for a successful notarization visit.

### Visitor Application Requirements

All notaries visiting Oklahoma state prisons must complete the DOC Visitor Application Form. This process includes:

- **Background Check**: A comprehensive background check is conducted for all visitors 18 and older. Approval can take up to 90 days, though notary visits may qualify for expedited processing in some circumstances.
- **Photo Identification Submission**: A copy of your valid government-issued photo ID must accompany the application.
- **Application Submission**: Paper applications should be addressed to the Visitation Unit at the specific facility or mailed to Oklahoma Department of Corrections, Attn: Visitation Unit, P.O. Box 11400, Oklahoma City, OK 73136-0400.

### Notary Credentials Verification

Notaries visiting Oklahoma correctional facilities must provide:
- Valid Oklahoma notary commission certificate
- Current photo identification (driver's license or state ID)
- Notary seal and journal for inspection by facility staff
- Documentation of the specific notarization purpose

Facility staff may inspect all notary materials, including seals, journals, and documents to be notarized, before allowing entry.

## Scheduling and Clearance Procedures

Proper scheduling is critical for jail and prison notarizations. Oklahoma facilities do not accept walk-in visitors, including notaries.

### Advance Scheduling Requirements

- **All visits must be scheduled** – no walk-ins are accepted at Oklahoma DOC facilities
- **Schedule requests must be received by Wednesday at 1 p.m.** for weekend visits
- No changes or additions are permitted after the Wednesday deadline unless authorized by facility administration
- Notary visits typically occur during regular visitation hours or during designated special visit times

### Special Visit Classification

Notary visits may qualify as "special visits" under Oklahoma DOC policy, which includes:
- Professional service visits
- Attorney and legal support visits
- Clergy and ministerial visits

Special visits are typically scheduled for Mondays and Thursdays, though this varies by facility.

### County Jail Procedures

County facilities like David L. Moss Criminal Justice Center have different scheduling procedures than state prisons:
- Visitation must be scheduled in advance through the facility's scheduling system
- Notary visits may require coordination with the facility's legal visitation department
- Some county jails allow professional visits during extended hours compared to general public visitation

## Inmate Identification Requirements

Proper identification verification is a fundamental notary responsibility, and incarcerated signers present unique identification challenges that require specialized knowledge.

### Oklahoma DOC Inmate ID Cards

All inmates in Oklahoma state correctional facilities are issued official identification cards containing:
- Large color photograph with white or light background
- Full legal name and ODOC number
- Color-coded security level indicator
- Date of receipt at the assessment center
- Date of birth
- Barcode with ODOC number

These ID cards are considered valid primary identification for notarization purposes under Oklahoma law. The cards are printed on white PVC and must be displayed on the upper left torso of the inmate's clothing during notarization visits.

### Alternative Identification Methods

If an inmate has lost or damaged their ID card, replacement cards can be issued at the facility for a $5.00 fee. In rare cases where ID issues prevent immediate notarization, we can work with facility case managers to verify identity through:
- Consolidated Record Cards (CRC)
- Facility booking photographs
- Verification through the Law Enforcement Offender Inquiry System (ICON)

### County Jail ID Procedures

County facilities may have varying identification procedures. At David L. Moss and similar county jails, inmates typically have booking photos and wristbands that, combined with facility verification, can satisfy notary identification requirements.

## Security Protocols and Facility Procedures

Every correctional facility enforces strict security protocols that notaries must follow precisely. Failure to comply can result in immediate removal and potential permanent visitation restrictions.

### Pre-Entry Screening

All notaries must pass through comprehensive security screening:
- **Metal detector screening**: All visitors must pass through metal detectors
- **Pat-down searches**: Facility staff conduct pat-down searches on all visitors
- **Canine screening**: Drug detection dogs may screen visitors and vehicles
- **Warrant checks**: Law enforcement may run warrant checks on all visitors

### Permitted Items

Oklahoma DOC facilities strictly limit items visitors may bring:
- **Allowed**: Only identification, car key, and coins for vending machines
- **Prohibited**: Cell phones, electronic devices, recording equipment, bags, paperwork (except notary materials with prior approval), weapons, drugs, alcohol, tobacco products

Notaries should expect to secure all personal items in provided lockers before entering the facility.

### Visiting Area Conduct

Notary visits are conducted in designated visiting areas, typically:
- **Non-contact visiting areas**: Most notarizations occur through glass barriers with document pass-through slots
- **Supervised open areas**: Some facilities allow notarizations in supervised visiting rooms
- **Attorney visiting areas**: Legal professional visits may use designated attorney-client conference rooms

Physical contact between visitors and inmates is strictly prohibited except for brief greetings at the beginning and end of visits. All documents passed between notary and inmate are inspected by facility staff.

### Dress Code Requirements

Facilities enforce strict dress codes. Visitors, including notaries, should avoid:
- Revealing clothing, shorts, or skirts above knee length
- Sleeveless shirts or tank tops
- Clothing with offensive language or imagery
- Gang-related colors or symbols
- Hats and head coverings (except religious accommodations)

## Limitations: What We Cannot Notarize in Correctional Facilities

While we strive to accommodate all notarization needs, certain limitations apply to jail and prison notarizations due to facility security policies and legal restrictions.

### Document Restrictions

**Wills and Testamentary Documents**: Many facilities restrict or prohibit the notarization of wills due to security concerns about potential coercion or undue influence. We recommend consulting with the facility's legal department before scheduling will notarizations.

**Documents Requiring Multiple Witnesses**: Facilities typically restrict the number of visitors and the mixing of inmates with outside visitors, making documents requiring multiple independent witnesses impractical or impossible to execute properly.

**Documents with Restricted Content**: Facilities may review documents for security concerns and prohibit notarization of documents containing:
- Gang-related language or symbols
- Content deemed threatening to facility security
- Documents related to prohibited business activities

### Procedural Limitations

**Remote Online Notarization**: Remote online notarization is generally not available for incarcerated signers due to facility restrictions on internet access and electronic devices.

**After-Hours Emergencies**: While we offer expedited service when possible, facilities operate on strict schedules. True after-hours notarizations are rarely possible due to facility lockdown procedures.

**Interpreter Requirements**: If an inmate requires an interpreter for notarization, the interpreter must also complete visitor clearance procedures, which may delay scheduling significantly.

### Legal Limitations

**Undue Influence Concerns**: Oklahoma notaries must ensure signers are acting of their own free will. If we observe signs of coercion or undue influence, we are ethically and legally obligated to refuse notarization.

**Mental Competency**: Signers must demonstrate understanding of the document they are signing. If an inmate appears incapacitated, medicated to the point of impairment, or unable to comprehend the document, notarization cannot proceed.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### How long does the visitor clearance process take?

For Oklahoma DOC facilities, the standard visitor approval process can take up to 90 days. However, notary visits may qualify for expedited professional visitor status at some facilities. County jails like David L. Moss typically have faster clearance times, often 24-72 hours for professional visitors.

### Can you notarize documents if the inmate is in disciplinary segregation?

Inmates in disciplinary segregation have restricted visiting privileges. Attorney and clergy visits are typically permitted, but notary visits for general purposes may be denied. We recommend contacting the facility classification department to verify visiting privileges before scheduling.

### What forms of ID do you accept from inmates?

We accept the official Oklahoma DOC inmate ID card as primary identification. This card includes a photograph, ODOC number, name, and date of birth. For county jails, we work with booking photos and facility verification procedures.

### Can family members be present during the notarization?

Generally, no. Notarizations require the signer to appear before the notary without other parties present who might exert influence. Family members may wait in the lobby but typically cannot participate in the notarization itself.

### How do I get the documents to the inmate for signing?

You should mail or deliver the documents to the inmate in advance through the facility's standard mail procedures. The notary should not bring unsigned documents into the facility except for the specific pages requiring notarization. Contact us for guidance on document preparation specific to your facility.

### What if the facility denies my notary visit?

If a facility denies a notary visit, we will work with you to explore alternatives, which may include:
- Rescheduling during different visiting hours
- Coordinating through the inmate's case manager or attorney
- Exploring remote notarization options for the outside party
- Requesting a temporary furlough or court transport for notarization purposes

### Do you provide witnesses for documents requiring them?

No, notaries cannot serve as witnesses on documents they notarize. If your document requires witnesses in addition to notarization, you must arrange for witnesses who have also completed facility clearance procedures.

### Can you notarize documents for inmates awaiting transfer?

Inmates in transit or awaiting transfer to another facility present special challenges. We recommend completing notarizations before transfer is initiated, as tracking inmates during transport can be difficult. If a notarization is needed during a transfer period, coordinate with both the sending and receiving facilities.

### How far in advance should I schedule?

For Oklahoma DOC facilities, schedule at least 2-3 weeks in advance when possible. For county jails, 3-5 business days is typically sufficient. Expedited service is available for urgent matters, subject to facility availability and clearance procedures.

### What happens if the inmate refuses to sign?

Notarization is always voluntary. If an inmate refuses to sign, no fees are refunded for travel and scheduling time already expended. We recommend confirming the inmate's willingness to sign before scheduling the notary visit.
