## 12 Local SEO Questions Answered

### 1. Is homepage overmixed between services?

**Yes.** The homepage attempts to cover both process serving AND notary services without clear visual separation. This dilutes topical authority for both service lines.

**Recommendation:** Create distinct visual sections with H2 headers: "Process Serving Services" and "Mobile Notary Services". Consider testing a homepage that leads with the higher-margin service (currently unclear which drives more revenue).

### 2. Should process serving and notary have separate hubs?

**Yes, absolutely.** Currently notary is treated as secondary. These should be equal pillars:

- `/process-serving/` — Hub for all serving content
- `/notary-services/` — Hub for all notary content
- Current city pages should have BOTH: `/service-areas/tulsa/process-serving` AND `/service-areas/tulsa/mobile-notary`

### 3. Are county pages helping or diluting?

**Helping, mostly.** The 54 county pages have genuine unique content (2–7% similarity). They capture long-tail "[county] process server" searches.

**However:** County pages lack notary content, trust signals, and video embeds. They're underoptimized for conversion.

### 4. Stronger city vs county pages?

**City pages are weaker.** Most city pages (especially the 37 markdown locations) are 77%+ templated and under 1,000 words. County pages average 1,567 words with more unique content.

**Verdict:** County pages are currently stronger. City pages need expansion to match county quality.

### 5. Which pages cannibalize?

**17 year-suffix pages cannibalize root pages:**
- `/oklahoma-process-server-pricing-2025` vs `/oklahoma-process-server-pricing`
- `/oklahoma-process-server-faq-2025` vs `/oklahoma-process-server-faq`
- And 15 more year-suffixed duplicates

**Also cannibalizing:**
- `/broken-arrow-process-server` vs `/service-areas/broken-arrow`
