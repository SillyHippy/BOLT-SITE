## What Is a Registered Agent?

A **registered agent** (also called a statutory agent or resident agent) is a person or business entity designated to receive official legal documents and government correspondence on behalf of your business. Every formal business entity operating in Oklahoma is required by law to maintain a registered agent.

### What Does a Registered Agent Do?

Your registered agent serves as the official point of contact between your business and the state of Oklahoma. The primary responsibilities include:

- **Accepting Service of Process**: Receiving lawsuits, subpoenas, and other legal documents when your business is involved in litigation
- **Receiving State Correspondence**: Accepting official notices from the Oklahoma Secretary of State, including annual report reminders and compliance notifications
- **Handling Tax Documents**: Receiving tax forms and notices from the Oklahoma Tax Commission
- **Maintaining Business Hours Presence**: Being available at a physical Oklahoma address during regular business hours to receive documents in person

## Oklahoma Statutory Requirements

Oklahoma law mandates that every business entity maintain a registered agent. The specific requirements are governed by **Oklahoma Statutes Title 18, Section 1022** (for corporations) and **Title 18, Section 2010** (for limited liability companies).

### Legal Requirements for Registered Agents in Oklahoma:

**Physical Address Requirement**
- Must maintain a physical street address in Oklahoma (P.O. boxes are NOT acceptable)
- The registered office must be open during regular business hours to accept service of process

**Availability Requirement**
- Must be available Monday through Friday during normal business hours (typically 9 AM - 5 PM)
- Must be present to accept documents in person when served

**Qualification Requirements**
The registered agent can be:
- An individual Oklahoma resident who is at least 18 years old
- A domestic or foreign corporation authorized to do business in Oklahoma
- A limited liability company (LLC) authorized to do business in Oklahoma
- A general or limited partnership, including limited liability partnerships

**Compliance Obligations**
- Must maintain a business office identical with the registered office
- Must promptly forward all received documents to the business entity
- Must notify the Secretary of State of any name or address changes

## Who Needs a Registered Agent in Oklahoma

### Oklahoma LLCs
Every limited liability company formed in Oklahoma must designate a registered agent. This includes:
- Single-member LLCs
- Multi-member LLCs
- Series LLCs
- Professional LLCs (PLLCs)

### Oklahoma Corporations
Both C-corporations and S-corporations must maintain registered agents, including:
- For-profit corporations
- Non-profit corporations
- Professional corporations
- Close corporations

### Out-of-State Entities (Foreign Qualification)
If your business was formed in another state but operates in Oklahoma, you must:
- Register as a **foreign entity** with the Oklahoma Secretary of State
- Maintain an Oklahoma registered agent
- File a Certificate of Authority ($300 for LLCs, $300 for corporations)

**Common Scenarios:**
- Texas LLC doing business in Oklahoma
- California corporation with Oklahoma customers
- Delaware company with Oklahoma operations
- Online businesses selling to Oklahoma residents

### Limited Partnerships (LPs) and LLPs
Registered limited partnerships and limited liability partnerships must maintain registered agents in Oklahoma.

### Nonprofit Organizations
Oklahoma nonprofit corporations must designate registered agents just like for-profit entities.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Can I be my own registered agent in Oklahoma?
Yes, you can serve as your own registered agent if you meet the requirements: you must have a physical address in Oklahoma (not a P.O. box), be at least 18 years old, and be available during regular business hours. However, many business owners prefer professional services for privacy, reliability, and convenience.

### What happens if I don't have a registered agent?
If your business fails to maintain a registered agent, the Oklahoma Secretary of State may administratively dissolve your company. You could lose your authority to do business in Oklahoma, and you may be unable to sue or defend yourself in court.

### How much does it cost to change registered agents in Oklahoma?
The state filing fee is $25 for domestic entities (LLCs and corporations) when filed by mail, or $35 when filed online. Foreign entities pay $50. These fees are in addition to any registered agent service fees.

### How long does it take to change registered agents?
Online filings are typically processed within 1-2 business days. Mail filings take 7-10 business days. In-person filings take 3-5 business days.

### Is a registered agent the same as an owner?
No. A registered agent is not automatically an owner of the business. These are separate roles, though the same person can serve in both capacities.

### Can my accountant or attorney be my registered agent?
Yes, if they meet the Oklahoma requirements and agree to serve in that capacity. However, many professionals charge significant fees for this service, and dedicated registered agent services often provide better value.

### What is service of process?
Service of process is the formal delivery of legal documents notifying a business that it is involved in a lawsuit. This includes summonses, complaints, subpoenas, and court orders.

### Do I need a registered agent in every state?
No. You only need a registered agent in the state where your business is formed and in any states where you are registered to do business as a foreign entity.

### What if I move my business to a different Oklahoma address?
If you move, you can either update your registered agent address (if serving as your own agent) or maintain your professional registered agent service without any changes. Many businesses prefer the stability of keeping a professional registered agent when they relocate.

### How will I know if my business receives legal documents?
With Just Legal Solutions, you receive same-day email notifications whenever we receive documents for your business. You also have 24/7 access to your secure online portal to view and download documents.

### What types of documents will my registered agent receive?
Your registered agent may receive: service of process (lawsuits), subpoenas, tax notices, annual report reminders, Secretary of State correspondence, wage garnishment notices, and other official government documents.

### Can a company serve as its own registered agent?
No, a company cannot be its own registered agent. However, an individual within the company (such as an owner or executive) can serve as the registered agent if they meet the state's requirements.
