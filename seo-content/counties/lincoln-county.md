## Overview of Lincoln County Legal Services

Lincoln County, located in central Oklahoma approximately 40 miles east of Oklahoma City, serves as an important hub along the historic Route 66 corridor. The county's courthouse in Chandler handles a significant volume of civil and criminal cases as part of the 23rd Judicial District, which also includes Pottawatomie County to the south.

Process servers operating in Lincoln County benefit from the county's centralized geographic position and well-maintained transportation infrastructure. The Turner Turnpike (I-44) traverses the northern portion of the county, making service of process accessible for defendants traveling through or residing in the area.

**County Seat:** Chandler  
**Established:** 1891 (original county of Oklahoma Territory)  
**Population:** Approximately 33,000 (2020 Census)  
**Area:** 959 square miles  
**Judicial District:** 23rd Judicial District (Lincoln and Pottawatomie Counties)

## Lincoln County Communities

### Chandler – County Seat

**Chandler** serves as the county seat and primary governmental center for Lincoln County. The city is situated at the intersection of State Highways 18 and 66, making it easily accessible for process servers operating throughout the region.

Founded during the land run era, Chandler maintains a historic downtown district while providing modern municipal services. The community supports a population of approximately 3,000 residents and features a mix of residential neighborhoods, commercial districts, and agricultural operations.

Process servers will find Chandler's layout straightforward, with numbered streets and named avenues creating an organized grid pattern. The courthouse district on Manvel Avenue represents the focal point for legal services.

### Prague – Czech Heritage Community

**Prague** is a distinctive community located in southeastern Lincoln County at the junction of U.S. Highways 62 and 377. Founded in 1902 by Czech immigrants and named after the capital of Czechoslovakia, Prague maintains a strong cultural identity.

The city of approximately 2,300 residents celebrates its heritage through the annual **Kolache Festival**, held the first Saturday in May. This event attracts 25,000 to 30,000 visitors who consume an estimated 50,000 kolaches—traditional Czech pastries. Process servers should be aware of this major cultural event when planning service attempts.

**Notable Prague Landmarks:**
- National Shrine of the Infant Jesus of Prague (St. Wenceslaus Catholic Church)
- Jim Thorpe Birthplace Monument (Olympic gold medalist born near Prague in 1887)
- Bohemian Hall (ZCBJ Lodge No. 46) – Listed on the National Register of Historic Places

Process servers should note that Prague residents pronounce the town name as "Prayg" (rhyming with "vague") rather than the European pronunciation "Prog."

### Stroud – Route 66 Heritage

**Stroud** sits astride historic Route 66 in northern Lincoln County and represents one of Oklahoma's most significant Mother Road communities. The city developed during the oil boom of the 1920s and thrived as a service center for Route 66 travelers.

The **Rock Café** stands as Stroud's most famous landmark and holds the distinction of being listed on the National Register of Historic Places. Built in 1939 using sandstone leftover from Route 66 construction, the café gained international recognition when Pixar used proprietor Dawn Welch as the inspiration for the character Sally Carrera in the animated film *Cars* (2006).

**Stroud Route 66 Attractions:**
- Rock Café (114 W. Main St.) – Historic restaurant and *Cars* movie inspiration
- Glen Mae Motel – Vintage motor court architecture
- Henshaw's Phillips 66 Station – Classic Rock Frame station design
- Ozark Trail Obelisk Replica (dedicated 2020)

### Additional Communities

**Meeker** – Located in northern Lincoln County, Meeker serves as a regional trade center with a population of approximately 1,100. The community is known as the home of Carl Hubbell, the Hall of Fame baseball pitcher for the New York Giants.

**Davenport** – A small community in the northwestern portion of the county, Davenport developed along the Ozark Trail before Route 66's establishment.

**Agra** – Located in eastern Lincoln County, this small community maintains agricultural roots and provides service opportunities in rural residential settings.

### Private Process Server Registration

Private process servers in Lincoln County must register with the district court clerk and maintain current credentials. The Presiding Judge for Lincoln County conducts the private process server docket and approves registrations for qualified applicants.

**Registration Requirements:**
- Oklahoma residency or court-approved non-resident status
- Background check clearance
- Knowledge of Oklahoma service of process statutes
- Current registration fee payment

## Geographic Considerations for Process Serving

Lincoln County's geographic position along the Turner Turnpike creates unique service challenges. The county's diverse terrain ranges from rolling prairie in the north to more wooded areas in the south. Rural addresses may be located on unimproved roads or require significant travel from main highways.

**Key Transportation Routes:**
- **Turner Turnpike (I-44)** – East-west corridor connecting Oklahoma City and Tulsa
- **U.S. Highway 62** – Southeast diagonal through Prague
- **State Highway 18** – North-south route through Chandler
- **State Highway 99** – North-south route through Stroud
- **Historic Route 66** – Original alignment through Stroud and Chandler
