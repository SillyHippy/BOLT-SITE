## Oklahoma State Requirements for Process Service

Under **Title 12 of the Oklahoma Statutes**, specifically **§ 2004**, process must be served by qualified individuals who understand the hierarchy of service methods.

### Who Can Serve Process in Marshall County:

- **Sheriff or Deputy Sheriff** – Marshall County Sheriff's Office serves throughout the county
- **Licensed Private Process Server** – Licensed under 12 O.S. § 158.1 with statewide authority
- **Court-Appointed Person** – Specially appointed by a judge for specific cases

### Licensing Requirements

To serve process legally in Marshall County, private process servers must:

- Be at least **18 years of age**
- Be an Oklahoma resident for at least **6 months**
- Reside in the county or judicial district for at least **30 days**
- Be of **good moral character**
- Post a **$5,000 bond**
- Pay licensing fees ($35 county-only, $150 statewide)

**Note:** Prisoners, parolees, and those convicted of violent crimes or sex offenses cannot obtain process server licenses in Oklahoma.

## Methods of Service in Marshall County

### Personal Service (Preferred)

Physically delivering the summons and petition directly to the defendant. Under Oklahoma law, the defendant does not need to physically "accept" the papers—if they refuse them, the server may leave them in their immediate proximity after identifying the documents.

### Substituted Service

When personal service is not reasonably possible:

- Leave copies at the defendant's **dwelling house or usual place of abode**
- Deliver to a person of **suitable age and discretion** (15+ years old) who resides there
- Multiple personal service attempts and reasonable diligence are expected first

### Service on Business Entities

- An **officer, director, or managing agent** of the company
- The **registered agent** designated with the Oklahoma Secretary of State
- The Secretary of State when the registered agent cannot be located

### Service by Mail

Certified mail with return receipt requested is permitted for certain defendants. Commercial couriers such as FedEx or UPS may be used if they generate a signed receipt (12 O.S. § 2004.3).

## Critical Time Limits: The 180-Day Rule

Under **12 O.S. § 2004(I)**, service must be made within **180 days** after filing the petition. Failure to serve within this window can result in dismissal without prejudice. Courts may extend this deadline for "good cause" shown.

## Chickasaw Nation Jurisdiction in Marshall County

Marshall County lies entirely within **Chickasaw Nation** territory, creating unique jurisdictional considerations for process serving.

### Understanding Tribal Jurisdiction

Following the landmark **McGirt v. Oklahoma** (2020) decision, tribal courts maintain significant jurisdiction over legal matters involving Native Americans within Indian Country.

**Key Points:**

- Criminal matters involving Native Americans fall under tribal or federal jurisdiction
- Civil cases involving tribal members may be heard in Chickasaw Nation courts
- Family law matters involving Native American children invoke ICWA
- The Chickasaw Nation maintains its own court system

### Concurrent Jurisdiction

Some cases involve **concurrent jurisdiction** between state and tribal courts, including:

- Divorce proceedings between tribal members and non-Natives
- Child custody disputes with Native American parents
- Contract disputes involving tribal businesses

Process servers must verify which court has jurisdiction before attempting service.

## Proof of Service Requirements

Under **12 O.S. § 2004(G)**, servers must provide:

- **Name** of the person served
- **Date** of service
- **Place** of service  
- **Method** of service used

File proof promptly and before the defendant's response deadline. Non-sheriff/non-licensed servers must file an **affidavit** swearing to the service facts.
