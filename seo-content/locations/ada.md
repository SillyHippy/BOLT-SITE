## Frequently Asked Questions (Ada-Specific)

### 1. How quickly can you serve papers in Ada?
Standard service in Ada is typically completed within 1-3 business days depending on the recipient's availability. Same-day and rush service options are available with advance notice. All serves include GPS-verified proof of service.

### 2. What courts handle cases in Ada?
Cases in Ada are primarily filed in the Pontotoc County District Court at the Pontotoc County Courthouse (120 W 13th St, Ada, OK 74820). Some matters may also involve tribal courts depending on the parties and subject matter. Municipal courts handle city ordinance violations.

### 3. Do you serve eviction notices in Ada?
Yes, we serve all types of eviction notices in Ada and throughout Pontotoc County, including 5-day, 15-day, and 30-day notices to quit under the Oklahoma Residential Landlord and Tenant Act.

### 4. Can you serve papers at rural addresses in Pontotoc County?
Absolutely. We serve all of Pontotoc County including rural and unincorporated areas. Rural service may require additional time for locating addresses, but all serves are GPS-verified with court-ready affidavits.

### 5. What types of documents do you serve in Ada?
We serve all types of legal documents including civil summons, subpoenas, divorce papers, eviction notices, protective orders, small claims papers, garnishments, and more. All service complies with Oklahoma law (12 O.S. § 2004).

### 6. Does tribal jurisdiction affect process serving in Ada?
Following the McGirt v. Oklahoma decision, Ada falls within tribal reservation boundaries. While this primarily affects criminal jurisdiction, it can impact certain civil proceedings. State courts generally retain civil jurisdiction, but some cases may involve tribal courts. We understand these nuances and ensure proper service regardless of jurisdiction.
