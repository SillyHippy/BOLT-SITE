## Frequently Asked Questions (Lawton-Specific)

### 1. How quickly can you serve papers in Lawton?
Standard service is typically completed within 2-3 business days. Rush service (24-48 hours) is available for urgent matters.

### 2. Do you have experience serving papers at Fort Sill?
Yes, we regularly coordinate with Fort Sill Security Forces and legal offices. Off-post service at residences is often more efficient than on-post service.

### 3. Can you serve papers on tribal land or tribal members?
We can serve papers on tribal members. Tribal court jurisdiction may apply depending on location and circumstances. We coordinate with Comanche Nation when necessary.

### 4. What about service at the Comanche Nation Casino?
Yes, but coordination with casino security is required. We recommend scheduling service during business hours when security management is available.

### 5. How do you handle service at Cameron University?
We serve students, faculty, and staff. Dormitory service requires coordination with housing staff; off-campus student housing is more accessible.

### 6. Can you serve papers at Reynolds Army Health Clinic?
Military medical facilities require base access. We coordinate with base legal for on-post medical facility service.

### 7. What if the person lives in Cache or Elgin?
We serve all of Comanche County including smaller communities. Service to outlying areas may require additional time.

### 8. Do you provide notary services in Lawton?
Yes, mobile notary services are available throughout Comanche County. We can meet at courthouses, offices, or residential locations.

### 9. How does military deployment affect service?
Deployed service members may be unreachable. We can attempt service at last known address and document attempts for court.

### 10. How do I submit documents for Lawton service?
Documents can be emailed, mailed to our Tulsa office, or uploaded through our secure client portal. We handle all preparation and service.

## About Comanche County

Comanche County, established in 1901, has Lawton as its county seat. Home to Fort Sill, one of the largest military installations in the United States, the county has a significant military presence that shapes its economy and culture. The county has a population of approximately 122,000.

**Key Statistics:**
- County Seat: Lawton
- Population: ~122,000
- Area: 1,069 square miles
- Major Highways: I-44, US-62, US-281, OK-7, OK-36
- Notable: Home to Fort Sill and Cameron University
