## 12 O.S. § 2004: Oklahoma Service of Process for Debt Collection

### Understanding the Legal Framework

Oklahoma's service of process statute, 12 O.S. § 2004, establishes the procedures for valid service of legal documents. For debt collection agencies, understanding these requirements is essential for ensuring that service will be upheld by Oklahoma courts and support judgment enforcement.

**Key provisions of 12 O.S. § 2004 relevant to debt collection:**

1. **Personal Service Requirements**: Documents must be delivered personally to the defendant or left at their dwelling house with a person of suitable age and discretion residing therein
2. **Corporate Service**: Service on corporations must be made on registered agents or authorized representatives
3. **Substituted Service**: Alternative service methods when personal service cannot be accomplished
4. **Service Documentation**: Affidavit requirements and proof of service standards

Our process servers are trained specifically in the application of 12 O.S. § 2004 to debt collection matters, ensuring every service attempt meets statutory requirements.

### Supporting Default Judgment Applications

In uncontested collection cases, proper service is essential for obtaining default judgments. Our service documentation is designed to support default judgment applications, with detailed affidavits that meet the evidentiary requirements of Oklahoma courts.
