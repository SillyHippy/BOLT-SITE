## Bristow's Historic Significance

### Railroad Heritage

Bristow owes its existence to the railroad. Founded in 1898 as a depot town on the **St. Louis-San Francisco Railway** (Frisco), the city grew rapidly as a shipping point for cattle, cotton, and agricultural products from the surrounding Cross Timbers region.

The historic **Bristow Railroad Depot**, built in 1923 and now listed on the National Register of Historic Places, stands as a testament to the city's railroad heritage. This beautiful Mission Revival-style building now serves as a museum and event center, preserving Bristow's connection to the golden age of rail travel.

### Route 66 Era

When **Route 66** was established in 1926, it ran directly through Bristow, bringing automobile tourism and new economic opportunities. The city embraced its role as a service center for travelers, developing:

- **Motor courts and motels** for overnight guests
- **Gas stations and garages** for the emerging automobile culture
- **Restaurants and diners** serving hungry travelers
- **Tourist attractions** to entice passing motorists

Today, Bristow maintains its Route 66 heritage with vintage signage, historic buildings, and annual events celebrating the "Mother Road."

### Oil and Gas History

Like much of Creek County, Bristow experienced significant growth during Oklahoma's oil boom era. The nearby **Bristow Field** and surrounding oil production brought prosperity and population growth throughout the early 20th century. While the oil industry's prominence has shifted, petroleum continues to play a role in the local economy.

## Turner Turnpike: Vital Transportation Link

### History of the Turner Turnpike

The **Turner Turnpike**, named after Governor Roy J. Turner, opened in **May 1953** as Oklahoma's first turnpike. This 88-mile toll road revolutionized travel between Oklahoma's two largest cities:

- **Before the turnpike**: 3+ hour drive via winding two-lane highways
- **After the turnpike**: 90-minute connection at 75 mph

The Bristow interchange (Exit 197) provides the northern Creek County access point to this vital transportation corridor.

### Modern Turnpike Access

Today's Turner Turnpike features:

- **Electronic tolling** with PIKEPASS
- **Service plazas** at regular intervals
- **75 mph speed limit** for efficient travel
- **Multiple interchanges** including Bristow, Stroud, and Chandler

### Economic Impact

The Turner Turnpike has shaped Bristow's development:

- **Commuter community** for Tulsa workers
- **Tourism access** to Route 66 attractions
- **Business location** advantages for distribution
- **Property values** enhanced by transportation access
