## The Glenn Pool Oil Discovery

### The Birth of Oklahoma's Oil Industry

The **Glenn Pool Field**, located just north of Mounds, represents the discovery that launched Oklahoma into the petroleum age:

#### The Discovery Well
- **Date**: November 22, 1905
- **Location**: About 8 miles north of Mounds (present-day Glenpool)
- **Discoverers**: **Ida E. Glenn** and **Robert Galbreath**
- **Drilling Company**: C.E. Byrd and R.M. McFarland
- **Initial Production**: 75 barrels per day
- **Peak Production**: Over 120,000 barrels per day at height

#### Historical Impact

The Glenn Pool discovery transformed Oklahoma and the entire petroleum industry:

**Economic Transformation:**
- Produced over **325 million barrels** of oil during its lifetime
- Made **Tulsa the "Oil Capital of the World"**
- Established Oklahoma as a major petroleum producer
- Created enormous wealth and development throughout the region

**Population Boom:**
- Mounds grew rapidly to serve the oil field
- Tent cities sprang up overnight near drilling sites
- Workers flooded into Creek County from across the nation
- Infrastructure developed to support the petroleum industry

**Technological Innovation:**
- Introduced new drilling techniques
- Developed improved oil field equipment
- Pioneered pipeline construction
- Advanced petroleum engineering practices

### The Mounds Connection

Mounds served as a vital service center for the Glenn Pool Field:

- **Supply hub** for drilling operations
