## About Kiefer, Oklahoma

### A Strategic Crossroads Community

Kiefer is a small but strategically located community in northeastern Creek County, Oklahoma, with a population of approximately 2,000 residents. Positioned just 15 miles south of downtown Tulsa and 18 miles northeast of Sapulpa (the Creek County seat), Kiefer provides residents with suburban convenience while maintaining its small-town character and Creek County identity.

### Multi-County Location

Kiefer sits at a unique geographic intersection:

- **Creek County**: Primary jurisdiction for most residents
- **Tulsa County**: Directly north and east
- **Okmulgee County**: To the south

This tri-county location makes Kiefer a significant service point for legal matters spanning multiple jurisdictions.

### Transportation Access

Kiefer's location provides excellent connectivity:

- **U.S. Highway 75**: Major north-south corridor connecting to Tulsa
- **State Highway 117**: East-west connection through Kiefer
- **Close proximity** to Creek Turnpike (OK-364) for Tulsa access
- **Easy reach** to both Sapulpa and Tulsa courthouses

**Drive Times:**
- Kiefer to Sapulpa (Creek County Seat): ~25 minutes (18 miles)
- Kiefer to Creek County Courthouse: ~25-30 minutes
- Kiefer to Tulsa: ~20 minutes (15 miles)
- Kiefer to Glenpool: ~10 minutes (8 miles)

## Oil and Gas Industry Connections

### Glenn Pool Discovery

The **Glenn Pool Field**, located just north of Kiefer near Glenpool, represents one of the most significant oil discoveries in American history:

**Historical Significance:**
- **Discovered November 22, 1905**
- **Ida E. Glenn** and **Robert Galbreath** drilled the discovery well
- **Launched Oklahoma's oil boom**
- **Produced over 325 million barrels** in its lifetime
- **Made Tulsa the "Oil Capital of the World"**

### Creek County's Petroleum Heritage

While the Glenn Pool Field is primarily associated with Tulsa County, Creek County shares in this petroleum legacy:

- **Southern extension** of Glenn Pool into Creek County
- **Pipeline infrastructure** crossing county lines
- **Oil field workers** residing in Kiefer area
- **Mineral rights** held by Creek County property owners

### Modern Oil Industry

Current petroleum activity near Kiefer includes:
