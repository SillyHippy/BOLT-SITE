## About Sapulpa, Oklahoma

### A Historic Oklahoma Community

Sapulpa holds a unique place in Oklahoma history as one of the state's oldest communities and the seat of Creek County since 1907. Named after **Sah-pul-pha**, a Creek (Muscogee) leader who established a trading post and ranch in the area during the 1850s, the city grew from a small frontier settlement into a thriving community of approximately 21,000 residents.

### Strategic Location

Sapulpa's location just **14 miles southwest of downtown Tulsa** makes it an ideal residential community for commuters while maintaining its distinct identity. The city sits at the crossroads of major transportation routes:

- **Turner Turnpike (I-44)**: Direct access to Tulsa and Oklahoma City
- **Historic Route 66**: Iconic highway running through the heart of Sapulpa
- **State Highway 97**: North-south corridor
- **State Highway 66**: Parallel to Historic Route 66

**Drive Times:**
- Sapulpa to Tulsa: ~20 minutes (14 miles)
- Sapulpa to Creek County Courthouse: Located downtown
- Sapulpa to Bristow: ~20 minutes
- Sapulpa to Oklahoma City: ~75 minutes via Turner Turnpike

## Local Landmarks and Points of Interest

### Sapulpa Historical Context

Sapulpa's history is deeply intertwined with Oklahoma's development:

#### The Railroad Era
The **St. Louis-San Francisco Railway** (Frisco) arrived in Sapulpa in 1886, transforming the small settlement into a major shipping point. The railroad established Sapulpa as a key distribution center for agricultural products, cattle, and later, oil.

#### The Glass Industry
Sapulpa became known as the **"Glass Manufacturing Capital of Oklahoma"** due to its extensive glass production facilities. The **Saint-Gobain Glass** facility (formerly Anchor Glass) continues this tradition today, representing one of the area's largest employers.

#### Historic Route 66
**Historic Route 66** runs directly through Sapulpa, bringing tourism and preserving the city's connection to America's "Mother Road." The Route 66 corridor features:

- **Vintage motels** and service stations
- **Local diners** and restaurants
- **Antique shops** and boutiques
- **Annual Route 66 events** and car shows

### Notable Local Landmarks

#### Heart of Route 66 Auto Museum
Located in downtown Sapulpa, this museum celebrates the city's connection to America's most famous highway with classic car displays and Route 66 memorabilia.

#### Sapulpa Historical Museum
Housed in a former hotel building, this museum preserves and interprets Sapulpa's rich history from its frontier origins through the oil boom and beyond.

#### Berryhill Historical Society Museum
Located nearby, this museum documents the history of the Berryhill area and its significance to Creek County development.

#### Euchee Creek
The **Euchee Creek**, which runs through Sapulpa, represents an important geographical feature and namesake for the Euchee community in the area.
