## Heyburn Lake: Kellyville's Recreational Jewel

### Lake Overview

**Heyburn Lake** is the defining geographic feature of the Kellyville area. This **920-acre reservoir**, managed by the U.S. Army Corps of Engineers, was created by damming Polecat Creek in 1952. The lake serves multiple purposes:

- **Flood control** for the Polecat Creek watershed
- **Drinking water supply** for surrounding communities
- **Recreation** for thousands of annual visitors
- **Wildlife habitat** and conservation area

### Recreation Areas

Heyburn Lake features three developed recreation areas, all easily accessible from Kellyville:

#### Sunset Bay
Located adjacent to Heyburn Dam, **Sunset Bay** offers:
- **14 primitive campsites** for tent camping
- **Boat ramp** with courtesy dock
- **Vault restroom** facilities
- **Scenic sunset views** over the lake
- **Access to fishing** and water activities

*Distance from Kellyville: Approximately 4 miles (10-minute drive)*

#### Heyburn Park
The largest developed area, **Heyburn Park** features:
- **46 campsites** with water and electric hookups
- **50-amp service** available at select sites
- **Two-lane boat ramp** with courtesy dock
- **Swim beach** with picnic tables
- **Playground** for children
- **Two group shelters** for events
- **Restroom/shower facilities**

*Distance from Kellyville: Approximately 5 miles (12-minute drive)*

#### Sheppard Point
**Sheppard Point** provides:
- **Tent and RV campsites**
- **Equestrian-friendly sites** with tether poles
- **Multi-use nature trails** for hiking and horseback riding
- **Hot showers** and restroom facilities
- **Boat ramp** and courtesy dock
- **Swim beach** and playground
- **Group picnic shelters**

*Distance from Kellyville: Approximately 6 miles (15-minute drive)*

### Fishing and Outdoor Activities

Heyburn Lake is renowned for excellent fishing:

**Popular Species:**
- **Crappie** (the lake's signature species)
- **Largemouth bass**
- **Channel catfish**
- **White bass**

**Additional Activities:**
- **Water skiing** and jet skiing
- **Boating** (no horsepower restrictions)
- **Swimming** at designated beaches
- **Hiking** on lakeside trails
- **Wildlife viewing** and bird watching
- **Hunting** (6,000+ acre public hunting area)

### Historical Context

Heyburn Lake is named after the **town of Heyburn**, which existed near the current lake location from the 1880s until the 1920s:

- **Founded in the 1880s** along the Frisco Railroad
- **Named for Clay Heyburn**, a local figure
- **Post office operated** from 1911 to 1922
- **Abandoned** after Route 66 bypassed the town
- **Submerged** when the lake was created in 1952

The **"Heyburn Store"** continues to operate today, carrying on the community's name at a location on Historic Route 66.
