# GPS-Tracked Process Serving with Photo Evidence

Just Legal Solutions uses GPS-verified process serving with photo documentation to provide irrefutable proof of service attempts. This technology exceeds Oklahoma legal requirements and gives attorneys court-ready documentation that defendants cannot successfully challenge.

## What is GPS-Tracked Process Service?

Our process servers carry GPS-enabled devices that record exact time, date, and precise coordinates of every service attempt. Combined with photographic evidence, this creates an unassailable record of due diligence that Oklahoma courts accept without question.

### GPS Documentation Includes:

- **Exact latitude/longitude coordinates** — Pinpoint accuracy to the specific address
- **Precise date and timestamp** — Down to the second for each attempt
- **Address verification against official records** — Confirms service at the correct location
- **Complete route history to location** — Shows the path taken to reach the service address
- **Time spent at service location** — Documents how long the server waited or attempted contact

### Photo Evidence Includes:

- **Address verification photos** — Clear images of house numbers, business signs, or property identifiers
- **Timestamped images of location** — Proof the server was physically present
- **Property condition documentation** — Notes gates, security systems, or access issues
- **Substituted service recipient photos** — When serving a substitute (family member, coworker), we document their relationship to the defendant
- **Attempt documentation** — Photos showing no one home, business closed, or other relevant conditions

## Why GPS Tracking Matters for Your Case

### Eliminates "I Wasn't There" Defenses

Defendants often claim they weren't served or were at a different location. GPS coordinates with timestamps provide irrefutable proof of exactly where and when service occurred. This evidence is particularly valuable in:

- Contested divorce proceedings where service is disputed
- Debt collection cases where defendants deny receiving notice
- Eviction proceedings where tenants claim improper notice
- Business disputes where corporate defendants deny service on registered agents

### Reduces Contested Service Hearings

With GPS and photo evidence, defendants rarely challenge service. This saves attorneys time and clients money on unnecessary court appearances. When service is properly documented with GPS coordinates and photos, the burden shifts to the defendant to prove they were NOT served — a much harder position to defend.

### Satisfies Due Diligence Requirements

Oklahoma courts require "reasonable diligence" for substituted service under 12 O.S. § 2004. GPS tracking documents multiple attempts at different times, demonstrating compliance with statutory requirements. The GPS record shows:

- Attempts were made at varied times (morning, afternoon, evening)
- Attempts were made on different days
- The server actually traveled to the location (not just claimed to)
- Sufficient time was spent attempting service

### Provides Appellate Court-Ready Documentation

If your case is appealed, the appellate court will review the record for proper service. GPS-tracked service creates a clear, objective record that appellate courts find persuasive. The combination of GPS data, photos, and detailed affidavits provides multiple layers of proof.

## GPS + Skip Tracing Combined

When defendants actively evade service, we combine skip tracing with GPS verification for maximum effectiveness:

### Step 1: Skip Trace Locates Current Address

We use public records, database searches, and investigative techniques to find the defendant's current location. This may involve:

- Property ownership records search
- Voter registration database queries
- Professional license lookups
- Social media and online presence analysis
- Utility connection records (where legally accessible)
- Employment verification

### Step 2: GPS Verifies Server Arrival

Once a location is identified, our server travels to the address with GPS tracking active. The GPS record confirms:

- The server reached the specific address
- The time of arrival and departure
- The route taken to the location
- That sufficient time was spent at the location

### Step 3: Photo Documents Service Attempt

At the location, we document:

- The address (house number, apartment number, business name)
- The condition of the location (occupied, vacant, for sale)
- Any obstacles to service (gated community, security guard, no trespassing signs)
- The person served (if applicable) or evidence of attempted service

### Step 4: Multiple Attempts Tracked and Timestamped

For evasive defendants, we make multiple attempts at different times and days. Each attempt is GPS-tracked and photographed, creating a comprehensive record of due diligence. This pattern of attempts often satisfies courts for substituted service orders.

### Step 5: Comprehensive Affidavit Prepared

The final affidavit includes:

- GPS coordinates and timestamps for each attempt
- Photos with embedded metadata showing date, time, and location
- Detailed narrative of each attempt
- Description of the person served or why service was not possible
- Chain of custody for the documents

## Legal Standards and Compliance

### Oklahoma Statutory Requirements

GPS-tracked service exceeds the requirements of 12 O.S. § 2004, which governs service of process in Oklahoma. While the statute only requires an affidavit stating service was made, courts increasingly expect additional documentation for contested cases.

### Federal Court Standards

For cases in federal court (Northern District of Oklahoma, Eastern District of Oklahoma, Western District of Oklahoma), GPS tracking helps satisfy the strict due process requirements of Federal Rule of Civil Procedure 4.

### Tribal Court Acceptance

Tribal courts in Oklahoma, including Osage Nation and Muscogee (Creek) Nation courts, accept GPS-tracked service documentation. We maintain current knowledge of each tribal court's specific requirements.
