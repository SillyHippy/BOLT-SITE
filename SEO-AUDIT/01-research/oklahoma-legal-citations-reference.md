# Oklahoma Legal Citations Reference Guide

## Process Serving Statutes

### 12 O.S. § 2004 - Methods of Service

**Primary Methods of Service in Oklahoma:**

#### A. Personal Service (12 O.S. § 2004(A))
Personal delivery of the summons and petition to the defendant. This is the preferred method and provides the most certain jurisdiction.

**Requirements:**
- Server must be 18+ years old
- Cannot be a party to the action
- Must complete proof of service affidavit

#### B. Substituted Service (12 O.S. § 2004(B))
When personal service cannot be made, service may be made by:
- Delivering copy to a family member 15+ years old at dwelling
- Leaving copy at dwelling with someone of suitable age
- Delivering to agent authorized by appointment or law

**Key Limitations:**
- Must be followed by mailing copy to defendant
- Mailing extends time to answer by 3 days
- Not available for certain document types

#### C. Service by Publication (12 O.S. § 2004.3)
When defendant cannot be found after diligent search:
- Publish in newspaper of general circulation
- Published once a week for 3 consecutive weeks
- Service complete 10 days after final publication

**Requirements for Publication:**
- Affidavit showing diligent search conducted
- Court order authorizing publication
- Notice must include case caption and court information

### 12 O.S. § 2005 - Time Limit for Service

**General Rule:**
Summons must be served within **180 days** from filing date.

**Extensions:**
- Court may grant additional time upon showing of due diligence
- Motion must be filed before expiration of original period
- Service by publication may require different timing

**Consequences of Failure:**
- Action may be dismissed without prejudice
- Court may order continuation or dismissal
- New filing may be required

---

### 12 O.S. § 158.1 - Private Process Server Licensing

**Licensing Requirements:**
- Oklahoma resident
- 18 years of age or older
- No felony convictions (certain exceptions)
- Complete application to Oklahoma Secretary of State
- Post $10,000 surety bond
- Pay applicable fees

**Renewal:**
- Licenses valid for specified term
- Renewal requires updated bond and fee
- Continuing education may be required

**Verification:**
- License status can be verified through Secretary of State
- Licensed servers listed in public database
- PSL number format: PSL-YYYY-#

## Notary Public Statutes

### 49 O.S. § 101 et seq. - Notary Public Act

**Commission Requirements:**
- Oklahoma resident or employed in Oklahoma
- 18 years of age
- Complete application to Secretary of State
- Pay $25 application fee
- Take oath of office

**Duties and Powers:**
- Take acknowledgments
- Administer oaths and affirmations
- Certify affidavits and depositions
- Verify identity of signers
- Maintain notary journal (recommended)

**Maximum Fees (49 O.S. § 101):**
- Traditional notarial act: $5
- Remote online notarization: $25
- Travel fees: Reasonable and agreed upon

---

### SB 1028 - Notary Law Updates (2026)

**Key Provisions:**
- $10,000 surety bond requirement
- Criminal background screening mandatory
- Enhanced identification verification

**Background Check Requirements:**
- FBI fingerprint-based check
- Oklahoma State Bureau of Investigation check
- Must be completed before commission
- Renewal requires updated screening

---

### HB 2265 - Identification Standards

**Acceptable ID for Notarization:**
- Oklahoma driver's license
- Oklahoma ID card
- U.S. passport
- Federal or state government ID with photo
- Tribal ID card (if contains photo and signature)

**Credible Witness Alternative:**
- Two credible witnesses personally known to notary
- Each witness must have acceptable ID
- Witnesses cannot be party to document

## Marriage/Officiant Statutes

### 43 O.S. § 7 - Persons Authorized to Solemnize Marriages

**Authorized Officiants in Oklahoma:**
- Judges or retired judges
- ordained ministers
- ordained priests
- rabbis
- Other persons authorized by religious organization

**Universal Life Church:**
- Ordination valid in Oklahoma
- Must present credentials if requested
- Certificate of ordination should be kept

---

### 43 O.S. § 5 - Marriage License Requirements

**Obtaining License:**
- Apply at county court clerk's office
- Both parties must appear in person (exceptions for military)
- Present valid identification
- Provide social security numbers
- Pay filing fee

**Waiting Period:**
- No waiting period in Oklahoma
- License valid for 10 days from issue
- Ceremony must occur within valid period

**Age Requirements:**
- 18+ years: No parental consent required
- 16-17 years: Parental consent required
- Under 16: Court order required

**After Ceremony:**
- Officiant must sign and return license
- Return to issuing clerk within 10 days
- Failure to return is misdemeanor

## Court Filing Procedures

### Tulsa County District Court

**Filing Hours:**
- Monday-Friday: 8:00 AM - 5:00 PM
- Closed weekends and state holidays

**Filing Methods:**
- In-person at clerk's office
- E-filing for attorneys (OSCINA system)
- Mail (not recommended for time-sensitive)

**Required Documents:**
- Original document for filing
- Filing fee or fee waiver application
- Cover sheet (civil cases)
- Service copies (if applicable)

**File-Stamped Copies:**
- Clerk provides upon request
- Fee for certified copies
- Essential for service of process
