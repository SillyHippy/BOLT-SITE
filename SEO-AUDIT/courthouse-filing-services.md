## How Courthouse Filing Works

**Step 1: Send Your Documents**  
Email, fax, or courier documents to our office at 564 E 138th PL, Glenpool, OK 74033. Include clear instructions, case numbers, and any special filing requirements.

**Step 2: Review and Preparation**  
We verify:
- Proper case caption formatting per Oklahoma court rules
- Required signatures and notarizations
- Filing fee calculations (we advance court fees and bill you)
- County-specific formatting requirements
- E-filing availability and requirements

**Step 3: Courthouse Filing**  
We file your documents according to the schedule for that county. Emergency and same-day filing available for Tulsa County.

**Step 4: Proof of Filing**  
You receive:
- File-stamped copies of all documents
- Receipts showing case numbers and filing dates
- Proof of payment for court fees
- Electronic copies via email
- Physical copies via mail or courier (your choice)

## eFiling Integration

Many Oklahoma courts now accept electronic filing through the Oklahoma State Courts Network (OSCN). We can:

- Prepare documents formatted for efiling portals
- Ensure compliance with technical requirements
- Handle document conversion and upload
- Provide backup physical filing when electronic systems are unavailable
- File original documents when wet signatures are required

Courts currently accepting efiling include Tulsa County District Court, Oklahoma County District Court, and many municipal courts. We monitor efiling availability statewide.
