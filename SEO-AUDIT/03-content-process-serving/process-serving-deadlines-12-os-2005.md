# Oklahoma Process Serving Deadlines: Understanding 12 O.S. § 2005

**Critical time limits for filing and serving pleadings in Oklahoma civil litigation**

---

Time is of the essence in civil litigation. Missing a critical deadline can result in dismissal of claims, default judgments, or waiver of defenses. Title 12, Section 2005 of the Oklahoma Statutes establishes comprehensive rules governing when and how pleadings and other papers must be served and filed in civil actions. This guide examines the statutory deadlines, calculation methods, and consequences of non-compliance under **12 O.S. § 2005**.

## Overview of 12 O.S. § 2005

Oklahoma's service and filing requirements are designed to ensure that all parties receive timely notice of proceedings while maintaining efficient case progression. The statute coordinates with Section 2004's process serving methods to create a cohesive procedural framework.

> **Statutory Authority:** "12 O.S. § 2005 - Service and filing of pleadings and other papers"

Understanding these deadlines is essential for attorneys, paralegals, self-represented litigants, and process servers to avoid procedural defaults that could jeopardize their cases.

## When Service is Required Under Section 2005

### General Rule of Service

**12 O.S. § 2005(A)** establishes the foundational requirement that certain documents must be served on all parties:

> **Statutory Text:** "Every order required by its terms to be served, every pleading subsequent to the original petition, every paper relating to discovery required to be served upon a party, every written motion other than one which may be heard ex parte, and every written notice, appearance, demand, offer of judgment, designation of record on appeal, and similar paper shall be served upon each of the parties"

This comprehensive list includes:
- Orders requiring service
- Responsive pleadings (answers, counterclaims, cross-claims)
- Discovery materials (interrogatories, requests for production, depositions)
- Written motions (except ex parte motions)
- Written notices of all types
- Appearances
- Demands
- Offers of judgment
- Designations of record on appeal
- Any similar documents

### No Service Required for Initial Petition

The initial petition and summons are governed by **12 O.S. § 2004**, not Section 2005. The petition must be served according to the process serving methods outlined in Section 2004, including personal service, mail service, or publication.

## Methods of Service Under Section 2005

### Personal Service

Documents may be delivered personally to the attorney or party:

> **Statutory Text:** "Service under the provisions of this section may be made by delivering a copy of the document to the attorney or party"

### Service by Mail

Mail service remains the most common method for serving subsequent pleadings:

> **Statutory Text:** "Service under the provisions of this section may be made by... mailing it to the last-known address of the party or the attorney of the party"

**Requirements for mail service:**
- Use of first-class mail (unless otherwise specified)
- Addressed to last-known address
- Sent to attorney of record (if represented)
- Sent to party directly (if self-represented)

### Electronic Service (Contemporary Practice)

While Section 2005 was enacted before widespread electronic filing, Oklahoma courts now permit and often require electronic service through the Oklahoma e-Filing system. Documents filed electronically are automatically served on registered users.

## Time Computation Rules

### Standard Computation Method

**12 O.S. § 2006** (incorporated by reference) establishes how time periods are calculated:

1. **Exclude the Starting Day:** The day of the triggering event is not counted
2. **Count Every Day:** Including intermediate weekends and holidays
3. **Include the Last Day:** Unless it is a weekend or legal holiday

### Extension for Mail Service

When service is made by mail, Oklahoma follows the "mailbox rule" with specific extensions:

> **Statutory Text:** "Whenever a party has the right or is required to do some act or take some proceedings within a prescribed period after the service of a notice or other paper upon the party and the notice or paper is served upon the party by mail, three (3) days shall be added to the prescribed period."

**Practical Application:**
- If a response is due within 20 days
- And service was made by mail
- The response is actually due within 23 days

### Computation Examples

**Example 1: Answer Deadline Calculation**
- Service of summons and petition: January 1
- Answer due: 20 days after service
- Counting: January 2 through January 21 = 20 days
- Answer due: January 21

**Example 2: Mail Service Extension**
- Motion served by mail: January 1
- Response due: 15 days after service
- Mail extension: 3 days
- Total time: 18 days
- Response due: January 19

## Specific Deadlines for Common Documents

### Answers to Petitions

Under **12 O.S. § 2012(A)**, a defendant must serve and file an answer within:

> **Statutory Text:** "A defendant shall serve an answer within twenty (20) days after the service of the summons and petition upon the defendant"

**Exceptions and Extensions:**
- Mail service adds 3 days (total 23 days)
- Corporations served via Secretary of State: 40 days (**12 O.S. § 2004(C)(4)(d)**)
- Court may extend time upon motion for good cause

### Responsive Pleadings to Counterclaims and Cross-Claims

When a party files a counterclaim or cross-claim, the opposing party must respond within:

> **Statutory Text:** "A party served with a pleading stating a cross-claim against that party shall serve an answer thereto within twenty (20) days after the service upon that party. The plaintiff shall serve a reply to a counterclaim in the answer within twenty (20) days after service of the answer or, if a reply is ordered by the court, within twenty (20) days after service of the order, unless the order otherwise directs."

### Motions to Dismiss and Defensive Motions

Under **12 O.S. § 2012(B)**:

> **Statutory Text:** "Every defense, in law or fact, to a claim for relief in a pleading to which a responsive pleading is required shall be asserted in the responsive pleading, except that the following defenses may be made at the option of the pleader by motion..."

Motions to dismiss under Section 2012(B) must be:
- Served before responsive pleading
- Or included in responsive pleading
- Filed within the time permitted for responsive pleading

### Discovery Deadlines

Discovery documents have specific service requirements:

**Interrogatories:**
- Must be served within time limits established by court order or scheduling order
- Standard deadline: Within 120 days after commencement of action (unless extended)
- Responses due: 30 days after service

**Requests for Production:**
- Same time framework as interrogatories
- Responses due: 30 days after service

**Deposition Notices:**
- Must provide reasonable notice (typically 5-10 days minimum)
- Must be served on all parties

### Post-Judgment Motions

**12 O.S. § 2005.1** addresses service in divorce actions specifically:

> **Statutory Text:** "In any action for divorce, service of all motions filed after the entry of the divorce decree, including but not limited to motions to modify custody, visitation or child support provisions, shall be governed by the provisions of Section 2005 of this title."

## Filing Requirements and Deadlines

### Certificate of Service Requirement

**12 O.S. § 2005(B)** mandates proof of service:

> **Statutory Text:** "All papers requiring service shall contain a certificate of service showing the date and manner of service, the names and addresses of the parties or attorneys served, and the signature of the person serving the papers"

**Consequences of Missing Certificate:**
- Document may be stricken
- Deadlines may not be considered met
- Court may require re-service

### Electronic Filing Deadlines

For courts requiring electronic filing:
- Documents filed before midnight on the deadline date are timely
- Technical failures may excuse late filing upon motion
- Parties should file well before deadline to avoid technical issues

## Case Type-Specific Deadlines

### Small Claims Cases

Small claims proceedings under **12 O.S. § 1751 et seq.** have expedited timelines:
- Trial typically scheduled within 30-60 days of filing
- Discovery is limited
- Response deadlines may be abbreviated

### Family Law Cases

Domestic relations proceedings involve unique deadlines:
- Temporary orders hearings: Typically within 14 days
- Discovery cutoff: Usually 30 days before trial
- Parenting plan deadlines: Court-specific

### Probate Proceedings

Probate matters operate under specialized timelines:
- Notice to creditors: 2 months publication requirement
- Claims against estate: 2 months from first publication
- Inventory due: 2 months after appointment

### Workers' Compensation

Workers' compensation proceedings follow administrative timelines:
- Claim filing: 1 year from injury (2 years for occupational disease)
- Response to claim: 30 days
- Appeal of administrative order: 10 days

## Consequences of Missing Deadlines

### Default Judgments

Failure to respond to a complaint within the statutory timeframe results in default:

> **Statutory Text:** "If a party against whom a judgment for affirmative relief is sought has failed to plead or otherwise defend as provided by these rules... the clerk shall enter the default of the party." — **12 O.S. § 2015(A)**

**Setting Aside Default:**
Under **12 O.S. § 1031.1**, a defendant may move to set aside default upon showing:
- Meritorious defense to the claim
- Excusable neglect for failure to respond
- Timely motion (typically within 3 years)

### Waiver of Defenses

Under **12 O.S. § 2012(H)**:

> **Statutory Text:** "A defense of lack of jurisdiction over the person, improper venue, insufficiency of process, insufficiency of service of process, or failure to state a claim upon which relief can be granted... is waived if not raised in a Rule 12 motion or in the answer"

**Critical Implication:** Missing the deadline to raise certain defenses results in permanent waiver of those defenses.

### Dismissal for Failure to Prosecute

Under **12 O.S. § 2014**:

> **Statutory Text:** "If there has been no action of record for twelve (12) months, any party who has appeared or the court on its own motion may give all parties who have appeared thirty (30) days' notice of a motion to dismiss for failure to prosecute"

Failure to serve required documents and move the case forward can result in dismissal.

### Sanctions

Courts may impose sanctions under **12 O.S. § 2011.1** for failure to comply with discovery deadlines or other procedural requirements.

## Calculating Complex Deadlines

### Sequential Deadlines

When multiple deadlines interact:

**Example: Motion for Summary Judgment**
1. Motion served: January 1
2. Opposing party's response due: 15 days = January 16
3. Moving party's reply due: 5 days after response = January 21
4. If served by mail: Add 3 days to each deadline

### Rolling Deadlines

Some deadlines are triggered by events rather than fixed dates:

**Service of Expert Reports:**
- Triggered by close of fact discovery
- Due 60 days before trial (unless court orders otherwise)
- Rebuttal reports due 30 days after original reports

### Jurisdictional Deadlines

Certain deadlines cannot be extended:

**Notice of Appeal:**
- 30 days from final judgment (unless post-trial motion filed)
- Cannot be extended by agreement
- Failure is jurisdictional

## Practical Best Practices

### Calendar Management

1. **Immediate Calendaring:** Enter all deadlines upon receipt of documents
2. **Reminder System:** Set reminders at 7 days, 3 days, and 1 day before deadlines
3. **Buffer Time:** Aim to complete work 2-3 days before actual deadlines
4. **Weekend/Holiday Review:** Check whether deadlines fall on non-business days

### Service Documentation

1. **Retain Proof:** Keep copies of certificates of service
2. **Track Receipts:** For certified mail, retain return receipts
3. **Confirm Electronic Service:** Verify e-filing system confirms transmission
4. **Follow Up:** Contact recipient if proof of service is questioned

### Extension Requests

When deadlines cannot be met:

1. **Motion for Extension:** File before deadline expires
2. **Stipulated Extensions:** Agree with opposing counsel when possible
3. **Good Cause Showing:** Explain reason for delay
4. **Proposed New Deadline:** Suggest realistic alternative date

## Emergency and Expedited Procedures

### Ex Parte Proceedings

Certain matters may be heard without prior service:

> **Statutory Text:** "...every written motion other than one which may be heard ex parte..."

Ex parte matters include:
- Temporary restraining orders (TROs)
- Emergency custody orders
- Appointment of receivers in emergencies

**Notice Requirements:**
Even for ex parte matters, courts typically require:
- Efforts to notify opposing party
- Short-form notice of the hearing
- Full hearing scheduled shortly after emergency order

### Emergency Filing Procedures

When immediate filing is necessary:
1. Contact court clerk for after-hours procedures
2. Many courts have emergency contact numbers
3. E-filing systems typically available 24/7
4. Follow up with physical copies if required

## Conclusion

Compliance with **12 O.S. § 2005** and related procedural deadlines is fundamental to successful civil litigation in Oklahoma. The statute's framework ensures that all parties receive timely notice while maintaining the efficiency of the judicial system.

Key takeaways for practitioners:

1. **Calendar Immediately:** Enter all deadlines upon receiving or serving documents
2. **Understand Extensions:** Apply mail service extensions and weekend/holiday rules correctly
3. **Document Everything:** Maintain complete certificates of service for all filings
4. **Know the Consequences:** Understand that missing certain deadlines can be case-fatal
5. **Seek Extensions Proactively:** Request extensions before deadlines expire, not after

The complexity of Oklahoma's civil procedure deadlines requires diligent attention to detail. When in doubt, consult the Oklahoma Pleading Code, applicable court rules, and current case law to ensure compliance with all service and filing requirements.
