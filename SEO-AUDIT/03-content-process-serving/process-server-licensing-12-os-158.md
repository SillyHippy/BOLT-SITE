# Oklahoma Process Server Licensing: 12 O.S. § 158.1 Requirements

**Complete guide to obtaining and maintaining a process server license in Oklahoma**

---

Process serving in Oklahoma is a regulated profession requiring state licensure. Title 12, Section 158.1 of the Oklahoma Statutes establishes the comprehensive framework governing private process server licensing, including qualifications, application procedures, bond requirements, and renewal processes. This guide provides detailed information for individuals seeking to become licensed process servers in Oklahoma, attorneys hiring process servers, and litigants seeking to verify server credentials.

## Overview of Process Server Licensing in Oklahoma

Oklahoma requires private process servers to obtain a license from the district court before serving civil process. This licensing requirement ensures that individuals entrusted with delivering critical legal documents meet minimum standards of character, competence, and accountability.

> **Statutory Authority:** "Service and return of process of court documents may be by an authorized licensed private process server. The presiding judge of the judicial administrative district in which the county is located, or an associate district judge or district judge of the county as may be designated by the presiding judge, shall be authorized to issue a license to make service of process of court documents to persons deemed qualified to do so." — **12 O.S. § 158.1(A)**

## Who Must Be Licensed

### Licensing Requirement

Under **12 O.S. § 158.1(I)**, operating without a license carries criminal penalties:

> **Statutory Text:** "Any person who knowingly and willfully serves process in Oklahoma without a process server license issued by the State of Oklahoma or who holds himself or herself out to be a process server licensed by the State of Oklahoma when the person is not licensed as such shall, upon conviction, be guilty of a misdemeanor."

**Important Exception:** Out-of-state process servers licensed in another state may serve Oklahoma defendants with out-of-state court documents without obtaining an Oklahoma license.

### Authorized Service Providers

The following categories may serve civil process in Oklahoma:

1. **Licensed Private Process Servers** (Statewide License)
2. **Sheriffs and Deputy Sheriffs** (Exempt from licensing)
3. **Persons Specially Appointed by Court** (Case-specific)
4. **Out-of-State Licensed Servers** (For foreign court documents only)

## Qualifications for Licensure

### Basic Requirements

**12 O.S. § 158.1(B)** establishes five mandatory qualifications:

> **Statutory Text:** "Any person who is:
> 1. Eighteen (18) years of age or older;
> 2. Of good moral character;
> 3. Found ethically and mentally fit;
> 4. A resident of the State of Oklahoma for a period of not less than six (6) months; and
> 5. A resident of the county or judicial administrative district in which the application is submitted for a period of not less than thirty (30) days,
> may obtain a license by filing an application with the court clerk on a verified form to be prescribed by the Administrative Office of the Courts."

### Detailed Qualification Standards

**1. Age Requirement**
- Must be at least 18 years of age
- No upper age limit specified by statute
- Must provide proof of age (driver's license, birth certificate, passport)

**2. Good Moral Character**
- Subjectively evaluated by the licensing judge
- May include review of:
  - Criminal history
  - Civil litigation history
  - Professional disciplinary actions
  - General reputation in community

**3. Ethical and Mental Fitness**
- No specific testing required
- Judge may consider:
  - Application responses
  - References
  - Any documented behavioral concerns
  - Professional history

**4. State Residency (6 Months)**
- Must be Oklahoma resident for minimum 6 months
- Proof may include:
  - Voter registration
  - Driver's license issuance date
  - Lease agreements
  - Utility bills
  - Tax returns

**5. County/District Residency (30 Days)**
- Must reside in application county/district for 30 days
- Ensures local accountability and jurisdiction

### Disqualifying Factors

**12 O.S. § 158.1(B)** prohibits certain individuals from obtaining licenses:

> **Statutory Text:** "Any person who has been convicted of a violent crime, as defined in Section 571 of Title 57 of the Oklahoma Statutes, or a crime that requires the person to register pursuant to the Sex Offenders Registration Act in this state or another state shall be prohibited from applying for or obtaining a license to serve process in this state."

**Violent Crimes Under 57 O.S. § 571 Include:**
- Murder and manslaughter
- Rape and sexual assault
- Robbery
- Assault and battery with dangerous weapon
- Kidnapping
- Domestic abuse
- Certain drug trafficking offenses

**Additional Disqualifiers:**
- Previous license revocation (5-year bar)
- Second revocation (lifetime bar)
- False statements on application
- Current felony probation/parole (may be disqualifying)

## Application Process

### Step 1: Obtain Application Form

Application forms are available from:
- District Court Clerk's office
- Administrative Office of the Courts (AOC)
- Some county court websites

The form requires disclosure of:
- Personal identification information
- Residential history
- Criminal history
- Previous process server licenses (Oklahoma or other states/counties)
- Employment history
- References

### Step 2: Determine License Type

Oklahoma offers two license types under **12 O.S. § 158.1(C)**:

#### Option 1: County-Specific License (Historical)
- Fee: $35.00 (initial)
- Renewal: $5.00 annually
- Authority: Single county only
- Renewal period: Annual after first year

#### Option 2: Statewide License (Current Standard)
- Fee: $150.00 (initial)
- Renewal: $15.00 per renewal period
- Authority: All counties in Oklahoma
- Renewal period: Every 3 years after first year

**Note:** Most applicants now pursue statewide licenses due to broader authority and cost efficiency.

### Step 3: Schedule Hearing Date

The application process requires a court hearing:

- Obtain hearing date from Court Clerk
- Hearing typically before Presiding Judge or designee
- Some counties hold hearings on specific days (e.g., Tuesday mornings)
- Tulsa County requires applications to be filed at least 5 working days before hearing

### Step 4: Complete Publication Requirements

**12 O.S. § 158.1(D)** requires public notice:

> **Statutory Text:** "Upon the filing of an application for a license, the court clerk shall give thirty (30) days of notice of hearing by causing the notice to be continually posted for thirty (30) days on the website of the county, or be posted in the courthouse. The applicant shall cause notice of the hearing to be made no less than twenty (20) days prior to the hearing one time by publication in a legal newspaper of the county..."

**Publication Requirements:**
- Published in legal newspaper of the county
- At least 20 days before hearing
- Applicant pays publication fee
- Proof of publication affidavit must be filed before hearing

### Step 5: Provide Notice to Officials

The Court Clerk must notify:
- District Attorney
- County Sheriff
- Oklahoma State Bureau of Investigation (OSBI)
- Administrative Office of the Courts

Notice must be delivered at least 20 days before hearing via electronic means or physical delivery.

### Step 6: Obtain Performance Bond

**Bond Requirements (12 O.S. § 158.1(E)):**

> **Statutory Text:** "...upon executing bond running to the State of Oklahoma in the amount of Five Thousand Dollars ($5,000.00) for faithful performance of his or her duties and filing the bond with the court clerk, the applicant shall be authorized and licensed to serve court documents in any county in this state."

**Bond Specifications:**
- Amount: $5,000.00
- Payable to: State of Oklahoma
- Purpose: Faithful performance of duties
- Term: Must be effective on hearing date
- Duration: 1 year (initial), 3 years (renewals)
- Provider: Licensed surety company

### Step 7: Prepare Supporting Documents

Required documentation typically includes:
- Two recent passport-size photographs
- Proof of Oklahoma residency (6 months)
- Proof of county residency (30 days)
- Valid government-issued photo ID
- Certificate of good standing from surety company (bond)
- Proof of publication
- Application fee payment

### Step 8: Attend Hearing

The hearing process:
- Presiding Judge or designee reviews application
- Determines if applicant meets qualifications
- Considers any protests filed
- Issues order granting or denying license

**Approval Conditions:**
- No protests filed, OR
- Applicant appears qualified despite protests
- All requirements satisfied

**Denial Consequences:**
> **Statutory Text:** "If, at the time of consideration of the application for the license, the presiding judge, associate district judge or district judge as is designated by the presiding judge determines that the applicant does not meet all of the qualifications necessary for a license, the applicant shall be prohibited from reapplying for a license to serve process for a period of not less than one (1) year from the date of denial."

## Protest Procedures

### Filing a Protest

Any Oklahoma citizen may object to an application:

> **Statutory Text:** "If any citizen of this state files a written protest setting forth objections to the licensing of the applicant, the district court clerk shall so advise the presiding judge or such associate district judge or district judge as is designated by the presiding judge, who shall set a later date for hearing of application and protest."

**Protest Requirements:**
- Must be in writing
- Must set forth specific objections
- Must be filed before hearing date
- No filing fee typically required

### Protest Hearing

If a protest is filed:
- Hearing rescheduled within 60 days
- Notice provided to all interested persons
- Applicant may present evidence
- Protester may present evidence
- Judge determines if applicant is qualified

## License Issuance and Content

### License Contents

Under **12 O.S. § 158.1(C)**, the license must contain:

> **Statutory Text:** "The license shall contain the full legal name, address, county in which the license was issued, a brief description of the licensee and a recent photograph of the licensee. The license shall state that the licensee is an officer of the court only for the purpose of service of process."

**Required License Elements:**
- Full legal name
- Residential address
- County of issuance
- Physical description
- Recent photograph
- Statement of limited authority (officer of court for service only)

### Carrying Requirements

> **Statutory Text:** "The license shall be carried by the licensee while on duty as a private process server."

When serving process, licensees must:
- Carry license on their person
- Present license upon request
- Not use badges or represent themselves as law enforcement

## Renewal Process

### Renewal Timeline

**Initial License:**
- Issued for 1 calendar year
- Must renew before expiration

**Subsequent Renewals:**
- Statewide licenses: Every 3 years
- Renewal fee: $15.00

### Renewal Procedures

Renewal applications must:
- Be filed before expiration
- Include updated information
- Include new bond (if required)
- Include renewal fee
- Include new photographs (if required)

**Late Renewal Consequences:**
- Name removed from authorized server list
- Cannot serve process until renewal approved
- Must reapply as new applicant if expired too long

### Renewal After Revocation

**First Revocation:**
- 5-year waiting period
- $1,000.00 renewal fee (instead of $15.00)

**Second Revocation:**
> **Statutory Text:** "If a second revocation occurs, the chief or presiding judge shall not allow an applicant to renew the license."
- Lifetime prohibition

## License Revocation

### Grounds for Revocation

The District Attorney or Attorney General may petition for revocation:

> **Statutory Text:** "The district attorney of the county wherein a license authorized under this act has been issued or the Attorney General may file a petition in the district court to revoke the license issued to any licensee, as authorized pursuant to the provisions of this section, alleging the violation by the licensee of any of the provisions of the law."

**Common Grounds for Revocation:**
- Fraudulent returns of service
- Service without actual delivery
- Misrepresentation of service attempts
- Criminal conviction while licensed
- Violation of process serving statutes
- Failure to maintain bond
- Misrepresentation as law enforcement

### Revocation Procedure

> **Statutory Text:** "After at least thirty (30) days of notice by certified mail to the licensee, the chief or presiding judge, sitting without jury, shall hear the petition and enter an order thereon."

**Revocation Hearing Process:**
1. Petition filed by District Attorney or Attorney General
2. 30-day notice by certified mail to licensee
3. Hearing before chief or presiding judge
4. Judge issues order (revocation or dismissal of petition)
5. Order entered on record

### Consequences of Revocation

- 5-year prohibition on reapplication (first revocation)
- Lifetime prohibition (second revocation)
- $1,000 renewal fee if reinstated after first revocation
- Name removed from statewide registry
- Potential criminal charges for subsequent unlicensed service

## Verifying a Process Server License

### Statewide Registry

**12 O.S. § 158.1(K)** requires the Administrative Office of the Courts to maintain a registry:

> **Statutory Text:** "No later than January 1, 2013, the Administrative Office of the Courts shall establish and maintain a statewide registry which shall contain a list of licensed private process servers."

**Verification Methods:**
1. **Online Registry:** Check AOC website for statewide registry
2. **Court Clerk's Office:** Each county maintains current list
3. **License Inspection:** Request to see physical license

### Information Available

The registry typically includes:
- Licensee name
- License number
- County of issuance
- License expiration date
- Status (active, expired, revoked)

### Red Flags

Signs a process server may not be properly licensed:
- Refuses to show license
- Cannot provide license number
- Name not appearing in registry
- License expired
- Demands payment before service
- Uses law enforcement terminology or badges

## Licensed vs. Unlicensed Servers

### Benefits of Using Licensed Servers

**Legal Compliance:**
- Service by unlicensed server may be invalid
- Default judgments may be set aside
- Courts may quash service

**Professional Accountability:**
- Bond provides financial protection
- License subject to revocation for misconduct
- Registry enables verification

**Knowledge and Training:**
- Licensed servers familiar with statutory requirements
- Understand proper return procedures
- Know territorial limitations

### Risks of Unlicensed Service

**For Attorneys:**
- Malpractice exposure
- Client damages
- Court sanctions
- Wasted litigation costs

**For Litigants:**
- Invalid service
- Dismissed cases
- Delayed relief
- Additional service costs

**For Unlicensed Servers:**
- Criminal misdemeanor charges
- Civil liability
- Injunction against future service

## Scope of Authority

### Geographic Authority

**Statewide License:**
- Valid in all 77 Oklahoma counties
- May serve any district court process
- Must file certified copy in additional counties (see below)

**County Registration:**
Under Tulsa County Rule 17 and similar local rules:
> "Upon an annual filing of a certified copy of a license issued pursuant to the provisions of this section and payment of a filing fee of ten (10) dollars to the Court Clerk of any county within this state, a licensed process server may serve process in that county for the district court having jurisdiction in that county."

### Limitations on Authority

**Permitted Activities:**
- Serving civil summons and petitions
- Serving subpoenas
- Serving discovery documents
- Serving post-judgment motions
- Executing certain court orders

**Prohibited Activities:**
- Executing arrest warrants
- Serving criminal process
- Executing writs of attachment
- Executing writs of execution
- Representing as law enforcement
- Carrying concealed weapons (unless separately licensed)

## Best Practices for Licensees

### Professional Standards

1. **Always Carry License:** Present when requested during service
2. **Maintain Bond:** Ensure continuous coverage
3. **Keep Current Address:** Notify court clerk of changes
4. **File Renewals Early:** Avoid lapse in authority
5. **Document Services:** Maintain accurate records
6. **Know the Law:** Stay current on statutory changes
7. **Avoid Conflicts:** Don't serve in cases with personal interest

### Return of Service

> **Statutory Text:** "Proof of service of process shall be shown by affidavit as provided for by subsection G of Section 2004 of this title."

Requirements:
- Sworn affidavit
- Date, time, and place of service
- Description of person served
- Method of service
- Any special circumstances

### Continuing Education

While not statutorily required, licensees should:
- Attend legal education programs
- Join professional associations
- Review statutory updates
- Network with court personnel

Oklahoma's process server licensing framework under **12 O.S. § 158.1** establishes important safeguards for the civil litigation system. The licensing process, while detailed, provides a clear pathway for qualified individuals to serve their communities while earning a living. By adhering to statutory requirements and maintaining professional standards, licensed process servers play a vital role in Oklahoma's justice system.
