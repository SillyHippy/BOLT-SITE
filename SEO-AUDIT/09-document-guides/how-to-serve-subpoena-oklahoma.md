# How to Serve a Subpoena in Oklahoma: Legal Guide

Serving a subpoena in Oklahoma requires understanding specific statutory requirements and procedures under **12 O.S. § 2004(C)(2)**. Whether you're an attorney, a pro se litigant, or a legal professional, proper subpoena service is critical to ensuring witness compliance and obtaining necessary evidence for your case.

This comprehensive guide covers everything you need to know about serving subpoenas in Oklahoma, including witness subpoenas, document subpoenas (subpoenas duces tecum), out-of-state procedures, and the unique requirements for Tulsa County and Creek County courts.

---

## Understanding Oklahoma Subpoena Law: 12 O.S. § 2004(C)(2)

### The Statutory Framework

Subpoena service in Oklahoma is governed by **Title 12, Section 2004(C)(2)** of the Oklahoma Statutes. This statute establishes the methods and requirements for compelling witnesses to appear and produce documents in legal proceedings.

Key provisions of **12 O.S. § 2004(C)(2)**:
- Subpoenas must be served by an authorized process server
- Specific service methods are required for valid subpoena service
- Witnesses are entitled to statutory fees and mileage
- Out-of-state subpoenas require special procedures
- Non-compliance can result in contempt of court

### What Is a Subpoena?

A subpoena is a legal document issued by a court that commands a person to:
- **Appear** as a witness at a deposition, hearing, or trial (witness subpoena)
- **Produce documents or evidence** (subpoena duces tecum)
- **Both appear and produce** documents (combined subpoena)

## Types of Subpoenas in Oklahoma

### 1. Witness Subpoenas (Subpoena Ad Testificandum)

A witness subpoena compels a person to appear and testify in a legal proceeding:

**Common Uses:**
- Compelling eyewitness testimony
- Requiring expert witness appearance
- Obtaining testimony from business representatives
- Securing appearance of parties in civil cases

**Requirements:**
- Must specify the date, time, and location of appearance
- Must identify the case and issuing court
- Must provide reasonable notice (typically sufficient time for witness to prepare)

### 2. Document Subpoenas (Subpoena Duces Tecum)

A subpoena duces tecum commands the production of documents, records, or physical evidence:

**Common Uses:**
- Obtaining medical records
- Securing business records and financial documents
- Acquiring employment records
- Getting police reports and investigative files
- Obtaining telephone records and digital evidence

**Requirements:**
- Must specifically describe the documents requested
- Must state the date, time, and place for production
- Should not be overly broad or burdensome
- May include inspection and copying provisions

### 3. Combined Subpoenas

Many subpoenas require both testimony and document production:
- Witness must appear at the specified time and place
- Witness must bring the requested documents
- Common in complex litigation and family law cases

## Who Can Serve a Subpoena in Oklahoma?

Under Oklahoma law, subpoenas must be served by an authorized individual. The server must be someone other than a party to the lawsuit.

### Authorized Subpoena Servers:

1. **Sheriff or Deputy Sheriff** - Of the county where service is made
2. **Licensed Private Process Server** - Licensed under **12 O.S. § 158.1**
3. **Court-Appointed Individual** - Specially appointed by a judge for a specific case
4. **Any Adult Over 18** - Who is not a party to the action (for some subpoena types)

**Important:** Parties to the lawsuit cannot serve subpoenas themselves. This includes attorneys representing parties—they must use a third-party server.

## Methods of Subpoena Service in Oklahoma

### Personal Service (Preferred Method)

Personal service is the most reliable method for serving subpoenas under **12 O.S. § 2004(C)(2)**:

**Requirements:**
- Hand delivery to the person named in the subpoena
- If the person is not available, substituted service at their dwelling may be permitted
- The server must inform the person that they are being served with a subpoena
- Documents must be physically handed to the person

**Proof of Service:**
- Affidavit of service completed by the process server
- Must include date, time, place, and manner of service
- Must identify the person served (name and physical description)

### Substituted Service

If personal service cannot be accomplished, substituted service may be permitted:

**Requirements:**
- Leave copies at the person's "dwelling house or usual place of abode"
- With a person **15 years of age or older** residing there
- Inform that person of the contents
- Document the name of the person who accepted service

### Service on Businesses and Organizations

Serving subpoenas on corporations, partnerships, and other entities requires special procedures under **12 O.S. § 2004(C)(3)**:

**For Corporations:**
- Serve an officer of the corporation
- Serve a managing or general agent
- Serve any agent authorized by appointment or law to receive service

**For Partnerships:**
- Serve a general partner
- Serve an authorized representative

**For Government Entities:**
- Serve the designated officer or agent
- Follow specific statutory requirements for state and federal agencies

## Service Deadlines for Subpoenas

### Reasonable Notice Requirement

Oklahoma law requires that subpoenas be served with **reasonable notice** before the required appearance or production date. What constitutes reasonable notice depends on the circumstances:

**General Guidelines:**
- **Depositions:** Typically 5-10 business days
- **Court hearings:** Follow court rules and scheduling orders
- **Trials:** Sufficient time for witness to arrange attendance
- **Document production:** Allow time to gather and organize records

**Factors Affecting Notice Requirements:**
- Complexity of documents requested
- Distance witness must travel
- Witness's availability
- Court-imposed deadlines

### Tulsa County and Creek County Procedures

Both Tulsa County and Creek County follow Oklahoma state law for subpoena service deadlines, with local court rules that may affect scheduling:

**Tulsa County District Court:**
- Check with the assigned judge's staff for specific scheduling requirements
- Some divisions have standing orders regarding subpoena timelines
- Allow extra time for service in busy dockets

**Creek County District Court:**
- Follow state reasonable notice requirements
- Contact the court clerk for local procedural preferences
- Schedule service to accommodate the court's calendar

## Witness Fees and Mileage in Oklahoma

### Statutory Witness Fees

Under Oklahoma law, witnesses subpoenaed to testify are entitled to statutory fees:

**Current Fee Structure:**
- **Per day fee:** Witnesses are entitled to a statutory daily fee for each day of attendance
- **Per mile fee:** Mileage reimbursement for travel to and from the proceeding

**Important:** The subpoena must be accompanied by a check or cash for one day's witness fee and round-trip mileage, or the witness may not be required to comply.

### How to Calculate Fees:

1. **Daily Fee:** Check current statutory rates (subject to legislative change)
2. **Mileage:** Calculated from the witness's residence to the court or deposition location
3. **Tolls and Parking:** May be reimbursable in some circumstances

### Non-Payment Consequences:

If witness fees are not tendered with the subpoena:
- The witness may refuse to appear
- The subpoena may be invalid
- You may need to re-serve with proper fees

## Out-of-State Subpoena Procedures

### Domesticating Foreign Subpoenas

If you need to serve a subpoena on a witness or for documents located outside Oklahoma, you must follow the **Uniform Interstate Depositions and Discovery Act (UIDDA)** or applicable interstate procedures.

**UIDDA Process:**

1. **Obtain the foreign subpoena** from the court where the case is pending
2. **File in the Oklahoma county** where the witness is located
3. **Request issuance** of an Oklahoma subpoena
4. **Serve the Oklahoma subpoena** following state procedures

**Steps for Out-of-State Subpoena Service:**

1. File a petition or application in the Oklahoma county where the witness resides
2. Attach the original out-of-state subpoena
3. Pay any required filing fees
4. Have the Oklahoma court issue a local subpoena
5. Serve the Oklahoma subpoena per **12 O.S. § 2004(C)(2)**

### Serving Oklahoma Subpoenas in Other States

To serve an Oklahoma subpoena in another state:

1. Contact the court clerk in the county where the witness is located
2. Follow that state's domestication procedures
3. Many states have adopted UIDDA procedures
4. Some states require commission or letters rogatory

### International Subpoena Service

For witnesses outside the United States:

- **Hague Convention procedures** may apply
- **Letters rogatory** may be required
- Consult with an attorney experienced in international discovery
- Allow significantly extended timelines

## Quashing a Subpoena in Oklahoma

### Grounds for Quashing a Subpoena

A subpoena may be quashed (invalidated) for various reasons:

**Procedural Grounds:**
- Improper service
- Insufficient notice
- Failure to tender witness fees
- Service by an unauthorized person

**Substantive Grounds:**
- Subpoena is overly broad or burdensome
- Documents are privileged (attorney-client, doctor-patient, etc.)
- Request seeks irrelevant information
- Compliance would be unduly oppressive
- Information is confidential or protected by law

### How to Quash a Subpoena

**Step 1: File a Motion to Quash**
- File with the court that issued the subpoena
- Serve the motion on the requesting party
- State specific grounds for quashing

**Step 2: Request a Hearing**
- Many courts require a hearing on motions to quash
- Be prepared to argue the legal basis for quashing
- Present evidence of burden, privilege, or irrelevance

**Step 3: Protective Orders**
- If the subpoena is not quashed entirely, seek a protective order
- Limit the scope of documents requested
- Modify the time or place of production
- Protect confidential information

### Objections to Subpoenas

Even if not moving to quash, a witness may file written objections:
- Objections must be served on the requesting party
- Common objections include privilege, burden, and relevance
- The requesting party may then file a motion to compel

## Frequently Asked Questions About Subpoena Service in Oklahoma

### Can I serve a subpoena myself in Oklahoma?

**No.** If you are a party to the lawsuit or an attorney representing a party, you cannot serve subpoenas yourself. You must use a sheriff, licensed private process server, or court-appointed individual.

### How much notice must I give for a subpoena?

Oklahoma law requires "reasonable notice." Generally:
- **Depositions:** 5-10 business days
- **Court appearances:** Sufficient time for witness preparation
- The specific circumstances of your case may require more or less notice

### What happens if someone ignores a subpoena?

Failure to comply with a properly served subpoena can result in:
- **Contempt of court** charges
- **Bench warrant** for the witness's arrest
- **Monetary sanctions**
- **Adverse inference** (court may assume the missing evidence would be unfavorable)

### Do I have to pay witness fees with the subpoena?

**Yes.** Oklahoma law requires that witnesses be tendered one day's statutory fee and round-trip mileage when served with a subpoena. Failure to provide fees may invalidate the subpoena.

### Can a subpoena be served by mail?

For most subpoenas in Oklahoma, personal service is required. Some limited exceptions may apply for:
- Agreements between parties
- Certain administrative proceedings
- Court-authorized alternative service

Consult with a process server or attorney about your specific situation.

### How long does a subpoena remain valid?

A subpoena is valid for the specific date and time stated in the document. If the witness fails to appear:
- The subpoena expires for that specific date
- A new subpoena must be issued for a rescheduled date
- Additional witness fees must be tendered

### Can I subpoena records from out of state?

Yes, but you must follow the **UIDDA** or applicable interstate procedures:
- Domestic the foreign subpoena in the state where records are located
- Follow that state's procedures for subpoena issuance
- Allow extra time for interstate procedures

### What's the difference between a subpoena and a summons?

- **Summons:** Initiates a lawsuit; notifies defendant they are being sued
- **Subpoena:** Compels testimony or document production during a pending case
- Different service requirements apply to each

### Can a subpoena request any documents?

No. Subpoenas must:
- Request relevant documents to the case
- Not be overly broad or burdensome
- Respect privileges (attorney-client, medical, etc.)
- Follow procedural requirements

### Do I need an attorney to issue a subpoena?

While attorneys commonly issue subpoenas, individuals representing themselves (pro se) can also request subpoenas:
- File a request with the court clerk
- Pay any required fees
- Arrange for proper service
- Tender witness fees
