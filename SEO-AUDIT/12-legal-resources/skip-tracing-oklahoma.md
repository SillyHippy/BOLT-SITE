**When someone doesn't want to be found, skip tracing becomes essential to completing legal service.**

This is where skip tracing—the art and science of locating people who don't want to be found—becomes invaluable. This guide explains skip tracing for legal professionals, attorneys, and individuals who need to locate someone for service of process in Oklahoma.

## What Is Skip Tracing?

### Definition

Skip tracing is the process of locating a person's whereabouts for any number of purposes, including service of process, debt collection, witness location, or asset recovery. The term "skip" refers to the person being searched for (having "skipped town"), and "tracing" refers to the process of tracking them down.

### Legal Context in Oklahoma

Skip tracing is legal and commonly used in Oklahoma for:
- Service of process (12 O.S. § 2004)
- Locating witnesses
- Debt collection (compliant with FDCPA)
- Asset location
- Missing persons investigations

### When Skip Tracing Is Needed

- Phone disconnected
- Avoiding service intentionally
- Home abandoned

### Oklahoma Legal Requirements

**For Service by Publication:**

Under **12 O.S. § 2004.3**, before service by publication is permitted, the plaintiff must demonstrate:

> "The plaintiff has made diligent search and inquiry to find the residence of the defendant, and that the defendant cannot be found within the state."

**What Constitutes Diligent Search:**
- Check of last known address
- Inquiry of postal service
- Check of telephone directories
- Inquiry of relatives
- Check of property records
- Check of employment records
- Skip tracing investigation

## Legal and Ethical Considerations

### Permissible Purposes

**Legal Uses in Oklahoma:**
- Service of process
- Locating witnesses
- Debt collection (FDCPA compliant)
- Asset recovery
- Insurance investigations
- Bail enforcement

**Prohibited Uses:**
- Stalking or harassment
- Identity theft
- Domestic violence
- Violating protective orders
- Illegal surveillance

### Fair Debt Collection Practices Act (FDCPA)

**For Debt Collection Skip Tracing:**
- Cannot contact consumer at inconvenient times
- Cannot contact consumer at work if employer prohibits
- Must cease contact if requested
- Cannot harass or abuse
- Must identify themselves

### Gramm-Leach-Bliley Act (GLBA)

**Financial Privacy:**
- Restricts access to financial information
- Requires permissible purpose
- Limits disclosure of non-public information

### Drivers Privacy Protection Act (DPPA)

**DMV Records:**
- Limits access to driver's license information
- Requires permissible purpose
- Oklahoma DPPA compliance required

### Oklahoma State Laws

**Private Investigator Licensing:**
Some skip tracing activities require a private investigator license in Oklahoma if conducted for third parties.

**Process Server Regulations:**
Skip tracing as part of process serving must comply with **12 O.S. § 158.1** licensing requirements.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Q: Is skip tracing legal in Oklahoma?

**A:** Yes, skip tracing is legal when conducted for permissible purposes such as service of process, debt collection (FDCPA compliant), or locating witnesses. However, skip tracing cannot be used for stalking, harassment, or violating protective orders.

### Q: How long does skip tracing take?

**A:** Simple database searches can be completed same-day. Comprehensive investigations typically take 3-7 days. Complex cases with intentional evasion may take 1-4 weeks. Rush services are available for 24-48 hour turnaround.

### Q: What information do I need to start skip tracing?

**A:** Minimum requirements are full legal name and last known location. Additional helpful information includes: date of birth, Social Security Number, previous addresses, employer, phone numbers, email addresses, and names of associates or relatives.

### Q: Can skip tracing find someone who changed their name?

**A:** Yes, professional skip tracers can often locate people who have changed their names through marriage, court order, or alias use. This requires cross-referencing multiple databases and analyzing historical data.

### Q: Is a Social Security Number required for skip tracing?

**A:** No, but it significantly increases success rates. Many locates can be completed with just a name and date of birth, or name and previous address.

### Q: What's the difference between skip tracing and a background check?

**A:** Skip tracing focuses on locating a person's current whereabouts. Background checks focus on a person's history (criminal, credit, employment). They use similar tools but have different objectives.

### Q: Can skip tracing find someone's current employer?

**A:** Yes, employment verification is a common skip tracing outcome. Methods include database searches, credit headers, LinkedIn profiles, and direct verification calls.

### Q: Is information obtained through skip tracing admissible in court?

**A:** Generally yes, if obtained legally. Information used for service of process must be verified and documented. The skip tracing report itself may be admissible depending on circumstances and authentication.

### Q: How much does professional skip tracing cost?

**A:** Basic locates start at $35-$75. Comprehensive searches range from $100-$250. Field investigations and surveillance cost $75-$150 per hour. Many process servers include basic skip tracing in their service fees.

### Q: What if skip tracing doesn't find the person?

**A:** Professional skip tracers provide detailed reports of all efforts made. If the person cannot be located after exhaustive search, you may proceed with service by publication (court approval required) or continue periodic searches.

### Q: Can I do skip tracing myself?

**A:** Yes, for simple locates. However, you won't have access to professional databases that contain the most current information. DIY skip tracing has lower success rates and takes more time.

### Q: Is skip tracing the same as hiring a private investigator?

**A:** Skip tracing is one service that private investigators offer. However, many licensed process servers also provide skip tracing. For service of process, using a server who also skip traces can be more efficient.
