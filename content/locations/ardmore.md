## About Carter County

Carter County, named for Captain Ben W. Carter, a Cherokee who lived among the Chickasaw, was established in 1907. With a population of approximately 48,000 (2020 census), the county seat is Ardmore. The area is known for its location along Interstate 35, the Arbuckle Mountains to the north, and Lake Murray State Park to the south.

**Key Statistics:**
- County Seat: Ardmore
- Population: ~48,000
- Area: 824 square miles
- Major Highways: I-35, US-70, US-77, OK-199
