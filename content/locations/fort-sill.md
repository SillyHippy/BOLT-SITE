## Local Service Tips for Fort Sill

### Military Base Service Protocols
- Active-duty service members are protected by the Soldiers and Sailors Civil Relief Act (SCRA)
- Base access requires advance coordination; visitors must present valid ID at the gate
- Service on base may require escort by military police or JAG representative
- JAG office can assist with locating service members on post
- Court orders served on military personnel may need to go through the chain of command
- See our [Military Base Service Guide](/guides/military-base-service) for full protocols

## Frequently Asked Questions (Fort Sill-Specific)

### 1. How do you serve papers at Fort Sill?
Serving papers on a military installation requires coordination with the base legal office (JAG). We contact the Staff Judge Advocate's office in advance, obtain necessary visitor authorization, and comply with all base access protocols. For active-duty service members, the SCRA may provide additional protections that affect timing and procedures.

### 2. Can you serve active-duty military personnel?
Yes, but with important caveats. Active-duty service members are protected by the Servicemembers Civil Relief Act (SCRA), which may allow them to postpone legal proceedings if military duties prevent participation. We ensure proper service while respecting these protections. Service is typically coordinated through the JAG office.

### 3. How quickly can you serve papers in the Lawton-Fort Sill area?
Standard service from Tulsa is typically 2-3 business days due to the 3-hour drive. Same-day service is available with advance notice. Military base service may require additional coordination time with JAG, typically adding 1-2 business days.

### 4. Do you serve divorce papers at Fort Sill?
Yes, divorce papers are among the most common documents we serve at military installations. Oklahoma divorce service requires personal delivery under 12 O.S. § 2004. For military divorces, there may be additional federal requirements. We work with your attorney to ensure compliance.

### 5. What about service at Reynolds Army Hospital?
Hospital service on Fort Sill follows both military protocols and HIPAA guidelines. We coordinate with JAG and hospital security to serve in appropriate areas while respecting patient privacy and military regulations.

### 6. Can you serve someone who lives in base housing?
Yes. Base housing service requires gate access, which we obtain through proper channels. We typically coordinate with the service member's unit or JAG office. Evening and weekend service of on-base housing is possible but requires prior authorization.

## About Comanche County

Comanche County, established in 1907, has Lawton as its county seat. The county is home to Fort Sill, one of the U.S. Army's premier training installations and the only active-duty artillery post in the United States. The Wichita Mountains Wildlife Refuge, one of the oldest managed wildlife facilities in the U.S., is located in the northern part of the county.

**Key Statistics:**
- County Seat: Lawton
- Population: ~120,000
- Area: 1,085 square miles
- Major Highways: I-44, US-62, US-277, US-281
- Notable: Home to Fort Sill U.S. Army Post; Wichita Mountains Wildlife Refuge; Cameron University
