## Frequently Asked Questions (Henryetta-Specific)

### 1. How quickly can you serve papers in Henryetta?
Standard service in Henryetta is typically completed within 1-2 business days. Same-day service is available with advance notice. Henryetta is about 75 minutes from our Tulsa base, making it easily accessible for routine serves.

### 2. Does tribal jurisdiction affect process serving in Henryetta?
Following the McGirt v. Oklahoma Supreme Court decision, Henryetta falls within Creek Nation reservation boundaries. This can affect criminal jurisdiction but generally does not change civil process serving procedures. State courts retain civil jurisdiction in most cases. We understand these nuances and ensure proper service.

### 3. Can you serve papers at rural addresses outside Henryetta?
Yes, we serve all of Okmulgee County including rural areas like Beggs, Schulter, Dewar, and unincorporated areas. Rural addresses may require additional time for location and access. We use GPS verification for all serves.

### 4. What courts handle cases in Henryetta?
Cases in Henryetta are primarily filed in Okmulgee County District Court at the county courthouse in Okmulgee (314 W 7th St). Some matters may also involve Creek Nation tribal courts depending on the parties and subject matter.

### 5. Do you serve eviction notices in Henryetta?
Yes, we serve all types of eviction notices including 5-day, 15-day, and 30-day notices to quit in Henryetta and throughout Okmulgee County. Oklahoma's Residential Landlord and Tenant Act governs these notices.

## About Okmulgee County

Okmulgee County was established in 1907 with Okmulgee as its county seat. The county has deep ties to the Muscogee (Creek) Nation, with Okmulgee serving as the Creek Nation capital. Henryetta, the county's second-largest city, was named after Hugh Henry, a coal mine operator, and was the birthplace of legendary athlete Jim Thorpe and World Series champion Troy Aikman.

**Key Statistics:**
- County Seat: Okmulgee
- Population: ~38,000
- Area: 702 square miles
- Major Highways: US-75, US-62, OK-52, I-44 (Turner Turnpike)
- Notable: Creek Nation capital; birthplace of Jim Thorpe; historic oil boom territory
