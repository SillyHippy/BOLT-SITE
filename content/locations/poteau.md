## Frequently Asked Questions (Poteau-Specific)

### 1. How quickly can you serve papers in Poteau?
Standard service in Poteau is typically completed within 1-3 business days depending on the recipient's availability. Same-day and rush service options are available with advance notice. All serves include GPS-verified proof of service.

### 2. What courts handle cases in Poteau?
Cases in Poteau are primarily filed in the Le Flore County District Court at the Le Flore County Courthouse (100 S Broadway St, Poteau, OK 74953). Some matters may also involve tribal courts depending on the parties and subject matter. Municipal courts handle city ordinance violations.

### 3. Do you serve eviction notices in Poteau?
Yes, we serve all types of eviction notices in Poteau and throughout Le Flore County, including 5-day, 15-day, and 30-day notices to quit under the Oklahoma Residential Landlord and Tenant Act.

### 4. Can you serve papers at rural addresses in Le Flore County?
Absolutely. We serve all of Le Flore County including rural and unincorporated areas. Rural service may require additional time for locating addresses, but all serves are GPS-verified with court-ready affidavits.

### 5. What types of documents do you serve in Poteau?
We serve all types of legal documents including civil summons, subpoenas, divorce papers, eviction notices, protective orders, small claims papers, garnishments, and more. All service complies with Oklahoma law (12 O.S. § 2004).

### 6. Does tribal jurisdiction affect process serving in Poteau?
Following the McGirt v. Oklahoma decision, Poteau falls within tribal reservation boundaries. While this primarily affects criminal jurisdiction, it can impact certain civil proceedings. State courts generally retain civil jurisdiction, but some cases may involve tribal courts. We understand these nuances and ensure proper service regardless of jurisdiction.
