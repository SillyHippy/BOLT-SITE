## Understanding Oklahoma Process Serving Laws

Custer County follows Oklahoma state law for service of process (Title 12 O.S. § 2004). Key requirements include:

- **Personal Service:** Documents must be delivered directly to the named defendant/respondent
- **Substituted Service:** If personal service fails, service may be made at the defendant's dwelling with a person of suitable age and discretion
- **Time Limits:** Most documents must be served within specific timeframes before court dates
- **Proof of Service:** A notarized return of service affidavit must be filed with the court

Our process servers are trained in proper service techniques that hold up in Custer County District Court.
