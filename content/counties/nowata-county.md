## About Nowata County, Oklahoma

### Geographic Overview

Nowata County is located in **northeastern Oklahoma**, bordering Kansas to the north. Covering just **581 square miles** (566 square miles of land and 15 square miles of water), it is the **smallest county in Oklahoma by total area**. The county is roughly divided into eastern and western halves by the **Verdigris River**, which flows south from Kansas into Rogers County.

The county's compact size actually works to the advantage of process servers—travel times between communities are minimal compared to larger Oklahoma counties. Major transportation routes include:

- **U.S. Highway 169** – North-south corridor through the county center
- **U.S. Highway 60** – East-west route intersecting at Nowata
- **State Highway 10** – Northern tier east-west connector
- **State Highway 28** – Eastern and northern route near Oologah Lake

### Communities We Serve

Nowata County contains **one incorporated city** and **five incorporated towns**, along with several unincorporated communities:

- **Nowata** (County Seat) – Population ~3,700. The commercial and government hub at the intersection of U.S. Highways 60 and 169.
- **South Coffeyville** – Population ~785. Located on the Kansas border, essentially a southern extension of Coffeyville, Kansas.
- **Delaware** – Population ~417. Named for the Eastern Delaware tribes who settled here after the 1867 treaty.
- **Lenapah** – Population ~293. Another community with Delaware roots ("Lenape" being the tribe's name), located in northern Nowata County.
- **Wann** – Population ~125. A small community that experienced brief oil boom growth in the early 1900s.
- **New Alluwe** – Population ~90. Located near the site of the former Alluwe town, relocated to accommodate Oologah Lake construction.

### Demographics and Economy

As of the **2020 Census**, Nowata County has a population of **9,322** residents. The local economy is primarily agricultural, with **cattle ranching and farming** (wheat, corn, oats, sorghum) forming the traditional economic base. Nowata County was also the site of some of **Oklahoma's earliest oil and gas development**, including the famous Cherokee Shallow Sand District—once one of the world's largest shallow oil fields.

## Historical Context and Local Knowledge

### A County Steeped in History

Nowata County was organized at **Oklahoma statehood in 1907**, carved from lands that were part of the **Cherokee Nation's Cooweescoowee District**. The name "Nowata" derives from a **Delaware word meaning "welcome" or "come here"**—a fitting sentiment for a county that has welcomed people for centuries.

### Notable Historical Facts

- The **Nowata County Courthouse** (built 1912) is listed on the National Register of Historic Places
- The county was home to the **Cherokee Shallow Sand District**, one of the world's largest shallow oil fields
- Notable residents have included **Roberta Campbell Lawson**, granddaughter of Delaware Chief Charles Journeycake
