## About Murray County, Oklahoma

**Murray County** is located in South Central Oklahoma and represents one of the state's most scenic and historically significant regions. Established in 1907 upon Oklahoma statehood, the county was named for **William H. Murray**, the ninth Governor of Oklahoma and a prominent figure in Oklahoma history known as "Alfalfa Bill."

### County Statistics

| **Attribute** | **Data** |
|---------------|----------|
| **Population** | ~14,000 (2020 Census: 13,904) |
| **County Seat** | Sulphur |
| **Total Area** | 424 square miles |
| **Founded** | 1907 |
| **Congressional District** | 4th |

### Major Communities

Murray County encompasses a mix of vibrant small cities, charming towns, and rural communities:

**Cities:**
- **Sulphur** – County seat and principal city (population ~5,000)
- **Davis** – Eastern Murray County community with strong tourism ties
- **Marietta** – Located in the southern portion of the county

**Unincorporated Communities:**
Hickory, Joy, Nebo, and numerous rural settlements throughout the county.

### Geographic Features

Murray County is renowned for its extraordinary natural beauty, featuring:

- **Chickasaw National Recreation Area** – Oklahoma's only national recreation area
- **Lake of the Arbuckles** – Pristine 2,350-acre reservoir
- **Turner Falls** – Oklahoma's largest waterfall at 77 feet
- **Arbuckle Mountains** – Ancient geological formation forming the county's northern boundary
- **Travertine Springs** – Natural mineral springs with therapeutic properties
- **Washita River** – Primary waterway flowing through the county

## Chickasaw Nation Jurisdiction

**Critical Information:** Murray County lies entirely within the historic territory and contemporary jurisdiction of the **Chickasaw Nation**, one of the Five Civilized Tribes of Oklahoma.

### Tribal Court Considerations

Process servers operating in Murray County must be aware of:

- **Tribal court jurisdiction** for matters involving Chickasaw Nation citizens
- **Concurrent jurisdiction** between state and tribal courts
- **Cross-deputization agreements** for law enforcement
- **Tribal sovereignty** affecting service of process procedures

The Chickasaw Nation maintains a significant governmental presence throughout Murray County, with tribal services, businesses, and cultural facilities serving both tribal citizens and the general public. Process servers should verify whether cases involve tribal parties, as this may affect where and how service must be effected.

### Chickasaw Nation Resources

- **Website:** [chickasaw.net](https://www.chickasaw.net/)
- **Tribal Court:** Handles domestic relations, juvenile, and civil matters involving tribal members
- **Law Enforcement:** Chickasaw Nation Lighthorpe Police Department

## Sulphur: County Seat and Gateway to Natural Wonders

**Sulphur** serves as the county seat, economic center, and cultural heart of Murray County. Located approximately 80 miles south of Oklahoma City and 110 miles north of Dallas-Fort Worth, Sulphur occupies a strategic position in South Central Oklahoma's tourism corridor.

### Historic Significance

Sulphur's history is intimately connected to the mineral springs that gave the town its name. The area was long known to Native American tribes for its healing waters before becoming a popular health resort destination in the late 19th and early 20th centuries.

### Chickasaw National Recreation Area

The **Chickasaw National Recreation Area (CNRA)** is the crown jewel of Murray County and one of Oklahoma's premier destinations:

| **CNRA Facts** | **Details** |
|----------------|-------------|
| **Total Area** | 9,899 acres |
| **Established** | 1902 (as Sulphur Springs Reservation) |
| **Lake of the Arbuckles** | 2,350 surface acres, 36 miles of shoreline |
| **Features** | Travertine springs, swimming holes, waterfalls |
| **Annual Visitors** | Over 1.5 million |

Originally established as a reservation to protect the mineral springs, the area was transferred to the National Park Service in 1906 and became **Platt National Park**—Oklahoma's only national park. In 1976, it was redesignated as the Chickasaw National Recreation Area, combining the original Platt National Park with the Lake of the Arbuckles area.

**Key Attractions Within CNRA:**

- **Travertine Nature Center** – Interactive exhibits and natural history displays
- **Travertine Creek** – Clear, spring-fed stream with multiple waterfalls
- **Little Niagara** – Popular swimming area and waterfall
- **Bromide Hill** – Historic observation point
- **Antelope and Buffalo Springs** – Historic mineral springs
- **Lake of the Arbuckles** – Premier fishing and boating destination

### Turner Falls Park

**Turner Falls** is arguably Oklahoma's most famous natural attraction and a defining feature of the Davis area in eastern Murray County:

| **Turner Falls Facts** | **Details** |
|------------------------|-------------|
| **Height** | 77 feet (Oklahoma's largest waterfall) |
| **Location** | 4 miles south of Davis, on Honey Creek |
| **Park Area** | 1,500 acres |
| **Features** | Swimming, hiking, camping, caves |
| **Season** | Typically open March through October |

The waterfall cascades into a natural swimming pool surrounded by stunning travertine formations. Turner Falls Park also features:

- **Caves** – Including the "Collings Castle," a 1930s-era stone structure built into the hillside
- **Hiking trails** – Network of trails offering views of the falls and surrounding terrain
- **Camping facilities** – Cabins, RV sites, and tent camping
- **Picnic areas** – Scenic spots throughout the park

### Downtown Sulphur

Sulphur's historic downtown district features:

- **Historic downtown buildings** – Early 20th-century architecture
- **Murray County Courthouse** – Center of county government
- **Local businesses and restaurants** – Serving residents and tourists

### Transportation Access

Sulphur's location provides good access for process servers:

- **U.S. Highway 177** – North-south route through Sulphur
- **State Highway 7** – East-west corridor connecting to I-35
- **State Highway 1** – Scenic route through the Arbuckles
