## Garfield County Overview

Garfield County, located in north-central Oklahoma, is anchored by the city of Enid, the county seat and economic hub of northwest Oklahoma. Named after President James A. Garfield, the county was opened to settlement during the Cherokee Strip Land Run of September 16, 1893. The county's economy is diverse, with strong agricultural roots, significant manufacturing presence, healthcare services, and Vance Air Force Base contributing to the local economy.

Enid serves as the primary trade and service center for a 14-county region in northwest Oklahoma and south-central Kansas, with over 142,000 people considering Enid their primary shopping location. The city's strategic location at the intersection of U.S. Highways 60, 64, 81, and 412 makes it a transportation hub for the region. The historic Garfield County Courthouse, an Art Deco masterpiece built in 1936, stands as a prominent downtown landmark.

## Courthouses in Garfield County

### Garfield County Courthouse (District Court)

**Address:** 114 West Broadway, Enid, OK 73701  
**Phone:** (580) 237-0232  
**Hours:** Monday-Friday, 8:00 AM - 4:30 PM  

The historic Garfield County Courthouse is an Art Deco architectural gem built in 1934-1936 with Works Progress Administration (WPA) funding. Listed on the National Register of Historic Places, the courthouse features murals by artist Ruth Augur and later additions by Native American artists Paladine and Burgess Roye. The building houses courtrooms, county offices, and the Garfield County Jail on the upper floors.

**Parking:** Street parking is available on Broadway and surrounding streets. Several public parking lots are located within a block of the courthouse. Metered parking is enforced Monday-Friday, 8 AM - 5 PM.

**Accessibility:** The courthouse is fully ADA compliant with elevator access to all floors.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### How do I file court documents in Garfield County?
Court documents are filed with the Garfield County Court Clerk at 114 W. Broadway, Enid, or electronically through the Oklahoma State Courts Network (OSCN) at www.oscn.net.

### What are the court filing fees in Garfield County?
Filing fees vary by case type. Contact the Court Clerk at (580) 237-0232 for current fee schedules or check the OSCN website.

### How do I obtain a marriage license in Garfield County?
Marriage licenses are issued by the Court Clerk's office. Both parties must appear with valid ID. The fee is approximately $50. No waiting period or blood test required.

### Where can I find property records for Garfield County?
Property and land records are maintained by the Garfield County Clerk at 114 W. Broadway, Room 105, Enid. Some records may be available online.

### What is the process for serving legal papers in Garfield County?
Legal papers may be served by the Sheriff's Office, a licensed private process server, or any person over 18 who is not a party to the case. The Sheriff's Office charges a fee for civil process service.

### How do I verify if someone is in the Garfield County Jail?
Contact the Garfield County Jail at (580) 548-2479 or call the Sheriff's Office at (580) 237-0244.

### What are the operating hours for the Garfield County Courthouse?
The courthouse is open Monday-Friday, 8:00 AM - 4:30 PM, excluding state holidays. The Court Clerk's office is open during lunch hours.

### Where can I park when visiting the Garfield County Courthouse?
Street parking is available on Broadway and surrounding streets. Metered parking is enforced Monday-Friday, 8 AM - 5 PM. Several public parking lots are located within one block of the courthouse.

### How do I get access to Vance Air Force Base for service?
Contact the Vance AFB Visitor Center at (580) 213-7111 for access procedures. You will need valid identification and may require sponsorship depending on the purpose of your visit.

### What is the best time to attempt service in Enid?
For residential service, evenings (5-8 PM) and weekends typically have higher success rates. For businesses, weekday business hours (9 AM - 5 PM) are best. Avoid shift change times near Vance AFB (7 AM, 3 PM, 11 PM) due to traffic congestion.

### How do I serve someone who lives on Vance Air Force Base?
Contact the Base Visitor Center at (580) 213-7111 to arrange access. You will need valid ID and may require sponsorship. Base housing areas have controlled access, and coordination with housing office may be necessary.

### Are there any areas in Garfield County with limited access?
Rural areas in western and northern Garfield County may have limited road access, particularly after heavy rain. Vance AFB has restricted access. Some industrial facilities have security protocols. Always confirm access before attempting service.

### What should I know about serving in rural Garfield County?
Many rural addresses use route and box numbers rather than street addresses. GPS navigation may be unreliable on unpaved roads. Cell phone coverage can be spotty. Confirm specific directions with recipients and allow extra travel time.

### Does Garfield County have any unique filing requirements?
Standard Oklahoma filing procedures apply. All filings must comply with Oklahoma Statutes and Oklahoma Supreme Court rules. Electronic filing is available through OSCN for most case types.

### How long does it take to get a court date in Garfield County?
Civil case scheduling varies by case type and court calendar. Small claims are typically scheduled within 4-6 weeks. Other civil matters may take 6-12 weeks depending on the judge's calendar. Contact the Court Clerk for current scheduling information.

### What is parking like at the Garfield County Courthouse?
Street parking is available on Broadway and surrounding streets. Metered parking is enforced Monday-Friday, 8 AM - 5 PM at $0.25 per hour. Several free public parking lots are located within one block of the courthouse. The courthouse square can be busy during business hours.

### Are there any local festivals or events that affect service?
The Garfield County Fair in September increases activity at the Chisholm Trail Expo Center. Downtown holiday events in November-December may affect parking. The Tri-State Music Festival in May brings visitors to the area. Check local event calendars when planning service.
