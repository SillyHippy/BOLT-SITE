## About Pittsburg County, Oklahoma

### County Overview

**Pittsburg County** is a vibrant county located in southeastern Oklahoma, nestled within the Choctaw Nation. Established in 1907 at Oklahoma statehood, the county was carved from part of the Choctaw Nation in Indian Territory and named after Pittsburgh, Pennsylvania, reflecting the area's significant coal mining heritage.

## McAlester: The County Seat

### Oklahoma State Penitentiary ("Big Mac")

No discussion of Pittsburg County is complete without acknowledging the **Oklahoma State Penitentiary (OSP)**, commonly known as **"Big Mac."** Established in 1908, this maximum-security facility is Oklahoma's oldest prison and houses the state's most serious offenders.

**Key Facts About OSP:**
- Opened in 1908 with 50 inmates transferred from Kansas
- Situated on 1,556 acres on the northeast side of McAlester
- Nicknamed "Big Mac" by locals and referenced as such in John Steinbeck's *The Grapes of Wrath*
- Home to Oklahoma's death row and execution chamber
- Site of the infamous 1973 prison riot — one of the most destructive in American correctional history
- Hosted the annual Prison Rodeo from 1940 to 2009, drawing up to 65,000 attendees at its peak

**Process Serving Note:** Serving legal documents to inmates at OSP requires adherence to strict Department of Corrections protocols. Process servers must coordinate with facility administration and provide valid identification. The prison's security protocols demand advance preparation and verification of all documentation.

### Italian Heritage in Krebs

Just three miles east of McAlester lies **Krebs, Oklahoma**, affectionately known as **"Oklahoma's Little Italy."** This community represents one of the most significant Italian-American settlements in the state.

**Historical Background:**
Italian immigration to the area began in the 1870s when coal mining companies recruited workers from southern and northern Italy to work in the Indian Territory mines. By 1910, Pittsburg County had 1,398 Italian residents — the largest Italian population in Oklahoma. Most immigrants came from the Piemonte region of northern Italy (73%) and the Abruzzi and Molise regions of southern Italy.

**Krebs Today:**
- Pete's Place — Operating for nearly 60 years, this iconic restaurant is operated by the Prichard family and serves authentic Italian cuisine
- The Italian Festival — Held annually since 1971 at the Pittsburg County Regional Exposition Center, featuring traditional Italian food, arts, and cultural celebrations
- St. Joseph's Catholic Church — Founded in 1885, serving the Italian community for over 135 years
- Historic downtown Krebs maintains its Italian-American character

### McAlester Army Ammunition Plant (MCAAP)

Southwest of McAlester lies one of the most significant military installations in the United States — the **McAlester Army Ammunition Plant (MCAAP)**. This facility plays a critical role in national defense and significantly impacts the local economy.

**Facility Overview:**
| Feature | Specification |
|---------|---------------|
| **Established** | May 20, 1943 (as Naval Ammunition Depot) |
| **Size** | 44,964 acres (70+ square miles) |
| **Buildings** | 2,891 structures |
| **Storage Igloos** | 2,426 magazines |
| **Workforce** | 1,400+ employees |
| **Roadway** | 397 miles |
| **Railroad** | 194 miles of track |

**Historical Significance:**
Originally commissioned as the **U.S. Naval Ammunition Depot at McAlester** during World War II, the facility was selected for its inland location, intersection of major highways and railroads, and sparsely populated surroundings. The Navy announced McAlester as a depot site on June 10, 1942. Construction began in August 1942 and was completed three months ahead of schedule, with commissioning ceremonies held on May 20, 1943.

The workforce peaked at over 8,000 employees during World War II, dropped to about 600 in the late 1950s, then surged again to 3,000 during the Vietnam War. On October 1, 1977, the facility transferred to Army control as part of the Single Manager for Conventional Ammunition initiative.

**Current Operations:**
- Produces, renovates, and stores conventional ammunition
- Manufactures approximately **one-third of all bombs used by the United States military**
- Produces Mark 82 general-purpose bombs, rockets, artillery shells, and various munitions
- Demilitarizes outdated ammunition through controlled disposal
- Houses the Defense Ammunition Center for training and research
- Second-largest ammunition facility in the Department of Defense

**TNT and Explosives History:**
The "TNT" connection to McAlester runs deep. The original Naval Ammunition Depot manufactured TNT (trinitrotoluene) and other explosives for the war effort. During WWII, the facility produced naval armaments, propellants, and explosives around the clock. Today, MCAAP continues this legacy, producing modern explosives including Tritonal (80% TNT, 20% aluminum powder) used in military ordnance.

**Process Serving Considerations:** MCAAP is an active military installation with restricted access. Process serving on the facility requires coordination with base security, proper identification, and advance clearance. Civilian process servers cannot access the installation without proper authorization and escort procedures.
