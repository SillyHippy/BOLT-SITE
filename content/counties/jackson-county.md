# Process Server Jackson County | Altus & Altus AFB Service

**Jackson County, Oklahoma**, presents distinctive challenges for process serving. Located in southwestern Oklahoma's agricultural heartland and home to **Altus Air Force Base**, this county requires specialized knowledge of military protocols, rural geography, and diverse communities. Our experienced process servers navigate these complexities daily, ensuring valid service throughout Jackson County.

---

## Jackson County Overview

Jackson County is situated in southwestern Oklahoma, bordering Texas to the south and west. Established on July 16, 1907, from portions of Greer County, it was named after President Andrew Jackson and Confederate General Thomas J. "Stonewall" Jackson.

| **County Statistics** | **Data** |
|----------------------|----------|
| **Population** | 24,792 (2020 Census)¹ |
| **County Seat** | Altus |
| **Total Area** | 804 sq mi (803 land, 1 water) |
| **Founded** | July 16, 1907 |
| **Time Zone** | Central (CST) |

### Geographic Boundaries

Jackson County is bordered by:
- **North:** [Greer County](./greer-county.md)
- **Northeast:** [Kiowa County](./kiowa-county.md)
- **East:** [Tillman County](./tillman-county.md)
- **South:** Wilbarger County, Texas
- **Southwest:** Hardeman County, Texas
- **West:** [Harmon County](./harmon-county.md)

The county is drained by the Red River and its tributaries, including the North Fork and Salt Fork of the Red River.²

## Altus Air Force Base Military Service

**Altus Air Force Base** represents the most significant factor distinguishing Jackson County for process serving. As the premier air mobility training location for the U.S. Air Force, this installation requires specialized procedures.

### Installation Overview

| **Altus AFB Statistics** | **Data** |
|-------------------------|----------|
| **Address** | 21 S. Ninth St, Altus AFB, OK 73523 |
| **Population** | ~5,000+ military and civilian personnel |
| **Host Wing** | 97th Air Mobility Wing |
| **Mission** | Air Mobility Training (C-17, KC-135, KC-46) |

### Military Service Requirements

Serving process on Altus AFB requires understanding federal military regulations and Oklahoma state law:

#### **Gate Access Procedures**

Altus AFB is a **controlled access installation**. Non-military personnel cannot simply drive onto the installation.³

**Visitor Control Center:**
- **Location:** Bldg. 21, 21 S. Ninth St, Altus AFB, OK 73523
- **Phone:** (580) 481-6300

**Requirements:**
- Valid photo ID (REAL ID-compliant driver's license or passport)
- Vehicle registration and proof of insurance
- Purpose of visit documentation

> ⚠️ **Important:** Visitors must have a sponsor or specific business purpose. Process servers should coordinate with the Base Legal Office in advance.

#### **Base Legal Office Coordination**

For service on active-duty military personnel, coordination with the **97th Air Mobility Wing Legal Office** is necessary. This process can take time, making early engagement critical for meeting deadlines.⁴

### Off-Base Military Housing

Many Altus AFB personnel live in surrounding communities:
- **Altus:** Largest off-base community (~5 miles from base)
- **Blair:** Small town north of Altus along US-283
- **Duke:** Community east of Altus
- **Martha:** Agricultural community north of Altus

## Why Jackson County is Unique

Process serving in Jackson County requires understanding three defining characteristics:

### 1. **Military Community Dynamics**

Altus AFB's significant presence creates:
- Frequent relocations requiring updated address verification
- Base access complexities for on-installation service
- Service member protections under the SCRA

### 2. **Agricultural Economy**

The rural landscape affects service through:
- Extended travel distances between residences
- Seasonal availability during planting and harvest
- Limited addressing in rural areas

### 3. **Southwestern Oklahoma Hub**

As the largest population center in far southwestern Oklahoma, Altus serves a multi-county region, often requiring coordination with cases spanning multiple jurisdictions.
