## Purcell: The Heart of Oklahoma

As the county seat, **Purcell** holds special significance in McClain County's legal and cultural landscape. Founded in 1887 at the junction of the Southern Kansas Railway and the Gulf, Colorado and Santa Fe Railway, Purcell served as a crucial gateway during the Land Run of 1889.

The city earned its nickname **"The Heart of Oklahoma"** due to its central location and historical importance as a transportation hub. Today, Purcell features:

- **Historic Downtown** – A charming Main Street district with preserved early-20th-century architecture, local businesses, and community events
- **Canadian River Valley** – The scenic river that forms the northern boundary of the city and provided the setting for the historic James C. Nance Memorial Bridge
- **Strategic Location** – Situated 35 miles south of Oklahoma City and 15 miles north of Norman, making it a convenient midpoint in the metro area
- **Cultural Heritage** – Home to the McClain County Courthouse, listed on the National Register of Historic Places, and Hotel Love, another historic landmark

The unique geography of Purcell—straddling the line between what was once Indian Territory and Oklahoma Territory—created a fascinating history during the prohibition era, with Lexington (just across the river) hosting numerous saloons while Purcell remained dry. This heritage lives on in the city's character today.
